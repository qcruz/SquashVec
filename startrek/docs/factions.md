# Starsleep — Factions Reference

**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Status:** Design document

---

## The Four Paths

When the player leaves Farpoint Station, they choose one of four faction affiliations.
This is not a permanent lock — cross-faction contracts, freelance work, and faction-switching
are all possible — but the starting choice determines the player's initial ship, crew,
resources, and mission types.

| Faction | Focus | Starting Position | Difficulty |
|---------|-------|------------------|------------|
| **The Hegemony** | Diplomacy, patrol, law | Centara Station; mid-tier ship | Medium — lots of rules but stable |
| **The Searchers** | Science, discovery, mapping | Archive platform; science-configured ship | Hard — limited combat, needs escorts |
| **The RedHawks** | Combat, escort, extraction | Redgate; well-armed ship | Medium — good at fights, bad at politics |
| **Independent** | Flexible, cross-faction | Farpoint itself; Varkis' ship | Very Hard — no support network |

## THE HEGEMONY

### Identity

The Hegemony is the closest thing the Reach has to a government. It maintains a fleet,
runs the transit lanes, manages the major stations, and has treaties with most inhabited
worlds. It is slow, bureaucratic, internally political, and largely well-intentioned.

Not evil. Just large. Large enough that individuals get lost inside it.

### What They Do Well

- Diplomatic missions — Hegemony ships carry legal authority that others don't
- Large-scale humanitarian response — they have the logistics
- Patrol and law enforcement — they know the lanes
- Crisis coordination — when three factions' ships are all needed, the Hegemony runs the meeting

### What They Do Badly

- Moving fast on intelligence that hasn't been verified through proper channels
- Off-the-books operations (they can want them; they can't officially do them)
- Anything requiring plausible deniability
- First contact with civilizations that have a reason to distrust large government fleets

### Starting Package

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Ship | Medium cruiser; well-equipped but regulation-standard |
| Crew | 4 named crew (1 Command, 1 Tactical, 1 Science, 1 Operations) |
| Starting credits | 8,000 |
| Starting Rep | Hegemony +30, Searchers +10, RedHawks +5, Independent +0 |
| Special | Diplomatic clearance (permits access to sovereign worlds) |
| Restriction | Mission reports required; some mission types unavailable without approval |

### Faction Missions

| Mission Type | Description | Unique To Hegemony |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Treaty Negotiation | Mediate disputes between systems or factions | Yes |
| Patrol and Enforcement | Maintain order on transit lanes | Yes |
| Humanitarian Response | Evacuate, supply, support civilian populations | Priority access |
| Intelligence Collection | Official intel gathering (with constraints) | Authorized version |
| Diplomatic Escort | Protect and support ambassadors | Yes |
| Emergency Coordination | Multi-faction crisis response | Hegemony leads |

### Salary and Advancement

Hegemony players receive a base salary (500 credits/cycle) regardless of mission success.
Commendations earn bonuses. Career flags can lead to formal reviews and salary suspension.

Promotion within the Hegemony follows the standard rank structure (Ensign → Captain).
Each promotion grants access to better ships, larger crews, and higher-stakes missions.
Reaching Commander unlocks "Diplomatic Core" access — the Hegemony's most sensitive work.

### The Hegemony's Blind Spot

The Hegemony officially doesn't negotiate with the RedHawks — they are unlicensed armed
vessels operating in Hegemony space. Unofficially, there is a permanent informal liaison.
This tension is a recurring source of missions.

## THE SEARCHERS

### Identity

The Searchers are a loose collective of scientists, explorers, archaeologists, and
cartographers who operate in the spaces between things — unmapped systems, anomalous
readings, dead civilizations. They are funded by a combination of research grants,
data sales to the Hegemony, private patrons, and practical finder's fees.

They believe that understanding what is out there is the most important work anyone
can do. They are often right.

### What They Do Well

- Anomaly investigation — better sensors, better training, better methodology
- First contact — patient, culturally fluent, genuinely curious
- Archaeological survey — standard practice
- Data collection and analysis — the best analysis tools in the Reach
- Long-range navigation — they've mapped more than anyone

### What They Do Badly

- Anything requiring immediate force
- Political leverage (they have academic standing, not governmental authority)
- Protecting their own research sites without outside help
- Deadlines — Searcher timelines are estimated

### Starting Package

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Ship | Scout or small cruiser; science-configured; exceptional sensors |
| Crew | 3 named crew (2 Science, 1 Engineering) |
| Starting credits | 5,000 |
| Starting Rep | Hegemony +10, Searchers +40, RedHawks +0, Independent +15 |
| Special | Research network (once per voyage, can query Archive for free Intel on destination) |
| Restriction | Combat-limited ship configuration; escort required for threat level High missions |

### Faction Missions

| Mission Type | Description | Unique To Searchers |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Anomaly Investigation | Study and document spatial/physical anomalies | Priority access |
| First Contact Protocol | Peaceful contact with previously unknown species | Yes |
| Archaeological Survey | Excavate and document ruins of extinct civilizations | Yes |
| Long-Range Mapping | Chart unmapped systems and update navigation data | Yes |
| Research Defense | Protect a research site from interference | Hired escort available |
| Data Recovery | Retrieve lost research from disabled vessels or ruins | Yes |

### Funding Model

Searchers don't earn salaries. They earn from:
- Mission completion fees (paid by the Archive or private patrons)
- Discovery bonuses (major anomaly, first contact: large one-time payment)
- Data sales to the Hegemony (negotiated per-transaction)
- Expedition grants (lump-sum pre-payment for long mapping voyages)

This means income is irregular but high-ceiling. A major discovery can fund years of operation.

### The Searcher Escort Dependency

Searchers cannot complete threat-level High or Critical missions without combat escort.
They can contract RedHawks or Hegemony ships:

| Escort Source | Cost | Tradeoff |
|--------------|------|----------|
| RedHawks | 15–30% of mission payment | Fast, effective; no questions asked |
| Hegemony | Formal permit required | Slower to arrange; political entanglement |
| Independent | Negotiated | Varies; relationship-dependent |

An escort contract does not make the escort crew part of your team — their loyalty is to
their own faction. Conflicts of interest can arise on ethically complex missions.

## THE REDHAWKS

### Identity

The RedHawks are the Reach's largest organized mercenary fleet. They take escort contracts,
extraction jobs, hostile-contact response, and anything else that requires being well-armed
and willing to use it. They are not criminals. Most of them have codes. Those codes are
just not the Hegemony's codes.

They have a structure — ranks, internal discipline, a base at Redgate — but the culture
is built on results and loyalty to your ship rather than to a bureaucratic hierarchy.

### What They Do Well

- Combat and tactical operations — best-equipped ships in the Reach (non-Hegemony)
- Escort and protection — reliable when contracted
- Extraction from hostile situations — their specialty
- Moving fast without paperwork — very good at this
- Training — RedHawk combat training is available for hire

### What They Do Badly

- Diplomatic missions — no official standing; doors close when they dock
- First contact — their approach tends to be read as threatening
- Long scientific operations — no patience, wrong equipment
- Operating in Hegemony core space without permits (friction is constant)

### Starting Package

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Ship | Light cruiser or corvette; combat-configured; best weapons in class |
| Crew | 4 named crew (2 Tactical, 1 Engineering, 1 Medicine) |
| Starting credits | 6,000 |
| Starting Rep | Hegemony +0, Searchers +5, RedHawks +50, Independent +20 |
| Special | Combat training bonus (+1 Tactical modifier on combat scenarios); arms trader contacts |
| Restriction | Diplomatic missions unavailable; Hegemony core space requires permits (costly) |

### Faction Missions

| Mission Type | Description | Unique To RedHawks |
|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Escort Contract | Protect a ship or convoy through dangerous space | Priority/cheapest |
| Hostile Extraction | Recover personnel from hostile control | Yes |
| Threat Response | Respond to active armed threat to a settlement | Yes |
| Bounty | Locate and detain specific individuals | Yes |
| Spec Ops | Intelligence-adjacent; deniable operations | Yes |
| Training Contract | Provide combat training to a crew or settlement | Yes |

### Payment Model

RedHawks are paid per contract. Rates are transparent (posted at Redgate).
Dangerous contract = high pay. Escort for a Searcher science mission = lower but steady.

Internal advancement in the RedHawks is based on contract performance, not time served.
A junior pilot who completes high-risk contracts advances faster than a veteran who takes
safe work.

### The RedHawk Permit Problem

RedHawks operating in Hegemony-controlled space technically require transit permits.
The Hegemony issues them, but slowly and with conditions (no offensive weapons primed,
no arrests without Hegemony authorization, etc.). Most RedHawks have a standing permit
that gets renewed annually — but can be revoked if they cause an incident.

A revoked permit is a serious career problem that requires either Hegemony Rep spend or
an Independent contact with Hegemony connections to repair.

## INDEPENDENT OPERATORS

### Identity

Independent operators own their ships. They answer to no faction's policies, follow no
fleet's mission assignments, and get paid by whoever has a job that needs doing. They
work across all factions, carrying things that don't go through official channels, finding
things that official ships aren't supposed to look for, and connecting parties who can't
be seen talking to each other directly.

The tradeoff: no salary, no institutional support, no repair credits, and no one comes
looking when you don't return.

### What They Do Well

- Cross-faction access — can work for anyone
- Black market and informal economy — natural lane
- Courier, salvage, private investigation — standard work
- Bringing parties together — the Reach's informal diplomats
- Operating in the grey areas — morally and legally

### What They Do Badly

- Anything requiring institutional authority they don't have
- Major combat without hired escort
- Long expeditions without a supply chain
- Political situations where faction standing matters

### Starting Package

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Ship | Varkis' ship — small but paid for; no weapons upgrades |
| Crew | 2 named crew (your choice of focus) |
| Starting credits | 3,000 |
| Starting Rep | Hegemony +5, Searchers +10, RedHawks +10, Independent +35 |
| Special | No faction restrictions; can take contracts from all four factions |
| Restriction | No salary; repairs out of pocket; weakest starting position |

### The Independent Advantage

Independent operators have one structural advantage: **optionality**.

Every voyage, they can select missions from any faction's board — mixing and matching
to maximize return. They can sell intelligence to the highest bidder. They can broker
introductions between factions that have political reasons not to deal directly.

A well-run independent operation in mid-career can be more profitable than any faction
affiliation. But it requires maintaining Rep with multiple factions simultaneously, which
means not burning any of them — a harder constraint than it sounds.

## Faction Interdependencies

### The Dependency Map

```
HEGEMONY ←──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────→ INDEPENDENT
  │  needs: off-book ops, grey market supplies,        needs: legal cover, repair access,
  │         intelligence not in official reports                 diplomatic muscle
  │
  │  HEGEMONY ←──────────────────── SEARCHERS
  │    needs: research data, discovery            needs: diplomatic clearance,
  │           intelligence for nav charts                 protection for sites
  │
  │  HEGEMONY ←─────────────── REDHAWKS
  │    needs: combat backup it                  needs: transit permits,
  │           can't officially request                   hegemony contracts
  │
SEARCHERS ←────────────────────────── REDHAWKS
  needs: combat escort                    needs: tech upgrades, sensor data,
         for dangerous missions                  intel on anomalies (for jobs)
  │
SEARCHERS ←────────────────── INDEPENDENT
  needs: rare materials,           needs: research data for
         specialized equipment               navigation, medical supplies
  │
REDHAWKS ←──────────────── INDEPENDENT
  needs: intel on contracts,   needs: combat protection
         black market parts           for dangerous routes
```

### Specific Cross-Faction Transactions

These are the mechanical interactions that generate cross-faction contracts and
player decisions:

| Transaction | Requester | Provider | Cost | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|----------|------|-------|
| Escort for research mission | Searchers | RedHawks | 15–30% of mission value | RedHawks set price; Searchers negotiate |
| Diplomatic clearance for sovereign world | RedHawks | Hegemony | Hegemony Rep spend or 2,000–10,000 credits | Takes 1 cycle to process |
| Off-books intelligence collection | Hegemony | Independent | 500–5,000 credits + plausible deniability guarantee | Hegemony agent contacts player privately |
| Rare materials (outside supply chain) | Searchers | Independent | Credits or Intel trade | Independent connects to Free Port traders |
| Research access to anomaly site | Hegemony | Searchers | Searchers share 25% of data | Formal partnership; documented |
| Arms/tech upgrade | RedHawks → sell | Any | Credits | Unlocked by RedHawk Rep ≥ 20 |
| Emergency combat backup | Hegemony | RedHawks | Paid contract + Hegemony permit extension | Hegemony cannot officially ask; routes through Independent broker |
| Reputation vouching | Any | Independent | Reputation cost (spends earned Rep points) | Independent can vouch for a faction contact |
| Repair and resupply (remote) | Independent | Hegemony or Searchers | Market rate credits | Hegemony docks, Searcher technical stations |
| Conflict zone medical support | All | RedHawks (with medic crew) | RedHawk contract rate | RedHawks have the access; Hegemony has the authority |

### The Broker Role (Independent Specialty)

Independent operators can act as **brokers** for cross-faction transactions.
A broker earns a fee (5–15% of the transaction value) by facilitating a deal that
neither party could arrange directly. Broker missions are their own mission type:

- **Introduction Broker**: Arrange first meeting between two factions who need
  each other but can't be seen collaborating. Requires Rep ≥ 10 with both parties.
- **Supply Broker**: Source materials that official channels don't carry.
  Risk: the materials may be contraband in some jurisdictions.
- **Information Broker**: Sell the same intel to two buyers without either knowing.
  Risk: if discovered, Rep loss with both. High reward.
- **Crisis Broker**: When a multi-faction response is needed, someone has to
  coordinate without political authority. Independent operators fill this gap.

## Changing Faction Affiliation

The player can switch factions. It costs:

1. **Formal resignation** from current faction (Rep loss: −20)
2. **Introduction** to the new faction (requires Rep ≥ 15 with them, or a voucher)
3. **Probation period**: 3 missions under new faction before full access is granted
4. **Ship reconfiguration** (optional but often necessary: costs credits)

**Notes:**
- Switching from Hegemony to RedHawks or vice versa has political visibility —
  both factions will notice
- Switching to Independent does not require introduction, but gives up all
  faction salaries immediately
- Switching back to a faction you left costs an additional −10 Rep (they remember)

## Faction Rep Levels and What They Unlock

All factions use the same Rep scale (−100 to +100):

| Range | Status | What's Available |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| −100 to −51 | **Enemy** | Hostile NPCs; faction ships will not dock with you; possible bounty |
| −50 to −1 | **Suspect** | Limited access; missions refused; no repairs at faction facilities |
| 0 to 19 | **Unknown** | Standard access; no bonuses |
| 20 to 39 | **Known** | Discounts; more mission types available; crew referrals |
| 40 to 59 | **Trusted** | Priority missions; high-value contracts; faction equipment unlocked |
| 60 to 79 | **Valued** | Senior contacts; classified mission access; faction-specific bonuses |
| 80 to 100 | **Pillar** | Top contracts; faction leadership introductions; unique equipment |

**Rep decay:** Rep above 40 with any faction slowly decays (−2 per voyage) unless
maintained through active missions with that faction. You cannot coast on old goodwill.

## The Faction Balance Problem

The game is designed so that maximum effectiveness requires cross-faction cooperation.
A pure Hegemony player who never works with RedHawks will fail at threat-level Critical
combat missions. A pure Searcher who won't deal with the Hegemony cannot access
sovereign-world research sites.

**The optimal play is to maintain Rep with 2–3 factions simultaneously**, which requires
choosing missions carefully and occasionally doing lower-paying work to maintain
relationships you'll need later.

A player who alienates two or more factions will find their mission pool shrinking and
their costs rising. This is recoverable but expensive in time.

## Faction Storylines (Planned)

Each faction has a specific long-arc storyline that runs over the five-year expedition:

| Faction | Storyline | Trigger |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Hegemony | A senior official is directing ships toward the anomaly zone for reasons they won't explain | Hegemony Rep ≥ 60 |
| Searchers | The Archive holds records of the previous civilization that was destroyed — and what destroyed it | Searchers Rep ≥ 60 |
| RedHawks | Two RedHawk ships are missing near the anomaly; their final transmission is three seconds of static | RedHawks Rep ≥ 60 |
| Independent | A Free Port contact has been selling coordinates to an unknown buyer — coordinates the player has visited | Independent Rep ≥ 60 |

These four threads converge on the same answer about the endgame encounter.
A player with high Rep in multiple factions will have more of the answer going in.
A player who burned their factions will face the encounter with less information.
