## Character Creation

At game start, the player creates their officer by making a small number of foundational
choices. These choices determine starting stat values and unlock certain background
options in early career scenarios.

### Academy Focus (pick one)

| Focus | Stat Bonuses | Starting Trait | Notes |
|-------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Command Track | Command +2, Diplomacy +1 | *Natural Authority* | Starts slightly ahead on crew relationship; behind on technical skills |
| Sciences | Science +2, Medicine +1 | *Analytical Mind* | Bonus to pre-resolution information gathering; starts with one Research free time completed |
| Engineering | Engineering +2, Science +1 | *Systems Thinker* | Ship condition starts higher on first tour; bonus to improvisation |
| Security/Tactical | Tactical +2, Command +1 | *Combat Ready* | Starts with higher physical condition; Tactical scenarios easier early |
| Medical | Medicine +2, Diplomacy +1 | *Healer's Eye* | Crew loyalty starts slightly higher; bonus to crew health management |
| Diplomatic Corps | Diplomacy +2, Science +1 | *Cultural Fluency* | Access to unique diplomatic options in first contact scenarios |

### Starting Stats (before Academy Focus bonuses)

All stats begin at 5 (on a 1–20 effective range after mods). Academy Focus adds bonuses.
Free time and career events develop from there.

## Traits

Traits are qualitative descriptors that modify how certain scenarios play out. They are
not stats — they don't add numbers directly. They gate options, modify partial success
outcomes, and occasionally change a scenario's full structure.

### Gaining Traits

- **Character creation:** one trait from Academy Focus
- **Free time:** specific activity sequences can unlock traits (see below)
- **Career events:** certain scenario outcomes grant traits permanently
  ("After the Tharkian Incident, you see threats differently." → *Threat Reader*)
- **Relationships:** deep loyalty with specific crew members can grant a reflected
  trait ("T'Vel has taught you to observe before acting." → *Vulcan Patience*)

## Free Time Activities — Detailed

Each free time slot is one choice from the available options. Options are always
available unless specifically locked by conditions (e.g., *Rest* is locked if there
is no rest period available; *Mentorship* requires an active mentor NPC relationship).

### Study
**Effect:** +1 to a chosen stat (Science, Engineering, Diplomacy, or Medicine).
Cannot raise a stat above the current rank ceiling through Study alone.
**Unlock:** Consistent Study in the same stat area unlocks the *Specialist* version of
that stat — slightly higher ceiling for that stat when the next rank ceiling increases.
**Flavor:** What you studied might be referenced in a future scenario option.

### Physical Training
**Effect:** +1 to Tactical (physical component); reduces injury severity from combat
and away mission scenarios; slows physical condition degradation.
**Note:** Tactical has two components — *combat instinct* (raised by Tactical scenarios)
and *physical fitness* (raised by Training). High Training doesn't make you a better
weapons officer; it makes you harder to put down.

### Station Practice
**Effect:** +1 proficiency at chosen station (separate from stats — a proficiency bonus
that applies specifically when working that station).
**Secondary effect:** Crew member who normally mans that station gains +2 loyalty
(you're working alongside them; you're learning their job; they respect it).
**Risk:** Overusing Station Practice signals to NPCs that you're preparing for a
specific scenario type — can sometimes telegraph your approach to NPC opponents.

### Crew Time
**Options within Crew Time:**
- *Mess Hall (open):* Small loyalty increase across all crew (+1 spread)
- *One-on-one with specific officer:* Larger loyalty increase with that officer (+5);
  reveals one piece of their backstory / motivation / hidden stat
- *Attend crew event (birthday, promotion, memorial):* Loyalty +3 spread; removes
  one grievance flag if any exist

**Philosophy:** Crew time is the most relationship-dense choice. Skipping it for long
periods accumulates small loyalty penalties that add up.

### Rest
**Effect:** Recover player fatigue (if fatigue system is active); no stat change.
**When to use:** After high-difficulty scenario sequences; before the endgame expedition;
when player condition is affecting roll modifiers.
**Opportunity cost:** Rest is the choice that gains nothing visible — which makes it
psychologically difficult to choose. Getting the player to rest when they should is
part of the game's design tension.

### Research
**Effect:** Invest a free time slot into investigating a specific open question (an
anomaly, a species you'll encounter, a system failure type). At the next scenario
that matches the research topic, you have one additional option available — an option
that wasn't there without the research.
**Risk:** If the scenario you prepared for doesn't appear during this tour, the
preparation carries forward but the slot is "spent" with no immediate return.
**Design note:** Research rewards anticipation and penalizes pure reactivity.

### Mentorship
**Requirement:** An active mentor NPC relationship (senior officer who has rated you
positively across multiple tours).
**Effect:** +1 to any stat, bypassing current rank ceiling; mentor's relevant trait
may be reflected to player (one-time acquisition).
**Limited uses:** Mentorship slots are scarce — a mentor can only actively develop you
once per tour. If you don't take the slot, it doesn't carry forward.
**Relationship cost:** If you consistently skip Mentorship when it's available, the
mentor reads it as disinterest; relationship degrades.

### Personal
**Effect:** Varies by character backstory and current arc.
**Design note:** Personal choices are the most narrative-heavy free time options.
They don't always have visible stat effects — sometimes they resolve a character
tension that has been building, unlock a backstory option in a future scenario, or
change how a specific NPC responds to you. They are the "roleplaying" option in a
primarily mechanical game.

## Development Paths

Over a full career, the player's stat profile will reflect their choices. There is no
"balanced" ideal — every profile has strengths and corresponding gaps. Three example
paths illustrate the range:

**The Career Diplomat:**
High Diplomacy, moderate Science, low Tactical.
- First contact scenarios are consistently successful
- Negotiation options are usually available and usually work
- Tactical scenarios require heavy reliance on crew; physical crises are uncomfortable
- Legacy score is high in peaceful first contacts and treaty outcomes
- Career ends badly if forced into sustained military conflict

**The Combat Veteran:**
High Tactical, high Command, low Medicine.
- Ship combat is reliable; crew responds well to clear orders
- A reputation for effectiveness that generates loyalty from security staff
- Medical crises are terrifying; crew health tends to suffer over long missions
- May have career flags from collateral damage accepted to win engagements
- Strong early-game when survival is at stake; struggles with the nuanced diplomatic
  scenarios of the five-year expedition

**The Engineer-Captain:**
High Engineering, high Science, moderate Command.
- Ship is always in better condition than anyone else's ship
- Technical and improvised solutions are consistently available
- Diplomatically slow; alien encounters are more awkward than they should be
- The ship almost always makes it home; the five-year mission is finished but not
  cleanly
- Underestimated by NPCs throughout the career; overdelivers consistently
