# The Ownership Economy

*What if every American owned a piece of the country they built?*

[STUB — expand in future session]

---

## Central Argument

The United States generates enormous national wealth through defense exports, trade
policy, and resource extraction — revenues that currently flow into the general fund
and are spent without any direct return to the citizens whose labor, taxes, and
geopolitical standing made those revenues possible. The American Sovereign Fund
redirects a defined share of those revenues into a permanently capitalized national
investment fund that grows over decades and distributes an annual dividend to every
eligible American citizen. It is not welfare. It is ownership.

---

## Key Evidence and Numbers to Build From

- U.S. arms sales in FY2024: approximately $265 billion. A 7.5% levy generates ~$20B/year
- Tariff revenue: $80–300 billion/year depending on trade regime. 20% share: $16–60B/year
- Norway Government Pension Fund Global: $1.6 trillion in assets, built over 30 years
  from oil revenue. The gold standard for what a revenue-funded sovereign wealth fund
  can become.
- Alaska Permanent Fund dividend: $1,000–$3,000/year per Alaskan since 1982. Most
  popular government program in Alaska by far. No serious politician has proposed
  eliminating it in four decades.
- Projected American Sovereign Fund dividend: $108/year at Year 15, $720 at Year 30,
  $2,174 at Year 50 (base case)
- The fund reaches $1.6T (Norway equivalent) at approximately Year 20 in the base case

## Tone Notes for When This Is Written

This article should feel like a genuine discovery — the reader should arrive at the
end thinking "why doesn't this exist already?" The Alaska comparison is the emotional
anchor: a program that has been paying dividends for forty years, that no politician
will touch, that Alaskans across every political identity defend fiercely. The national
version of that is not a radical idea. It is the obvious extension of something that
already works.
