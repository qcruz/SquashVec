## Central Argument

The short-run answer to rare earth dependency on China is domestic processing and allied
nation supply diversification — programs that can mature in 5–15 years. The long-run
answer is more radical: the asteroid belt and lunar surface contain concentrations of
rare earth elements, platinum group metals, and other critical materials that dwarf
anything available on Earth, with no geopolitical owner, no adversarial control, and no
competing civilian population to displace. The technology to reach them commercially is
not speculative — it is being built right now by companies with functioning hardware and
flight records. The question is whether the U.S. government will position itself as the
anchor buyer and framework setter that accelerates the commercial timeline, or whether
it will arrive after the market has already been structured by others.

## Key Evidence and Numbers to Build From

- The asteroid 16 Psyche (a metallic asteroid) contains iron, nickel, gold, and platinum
  group metals estimated at $10,000 quadrillion in face value — a number so large it
  illustrates why asteroid mining changes the nature of material scarcity rather than
  just the price
- More practically: near-Earth asteroid (469219) Kamo'oalewa is estimated to contain
  more platinum group metals than have been mined in all of human history
- The commercial space resource industry: $2.05B market in 2024 at 21.4% annual growth.
  Companies with actual hardware: AstroForge (launched first mission 2023), ispace
  (lunar landing attempt 2023), Karman+ and others in development
- The SPACE Act of 2015: already grants U.S. citizens legal rights to resources they
  extract in space. The legal foundation exists. The commercial infrastructure is developing.
- The Department of Economic Defense Accelerator model: $3–5B over 5 years in Type D
  strategic contracts (advance purchase commitments at 90% of terrestrial spot price)
  creates the revenue certainty that funds the commercial scale-up
- Terrestrial rare earth supply concentration risk score: 0.60 (Critical). Space-derived
  supply: 0.0 (no adversarial control possible under any scenario)

### The Advance Purchase Commitment Model

*TODO: The mechanism that makes the economics work. Commercial space resource companies
face the same first-mover problem as any capital-intensive new industry: investors will
not fund the scale-up until the revenue is certain; the revenue is not certain until
the scale-up happens. The government anchor buyer breaks this loop. The Department of
Economic Defense commits to purchasing materials at 90% of the terrestrial spot price
for a defined volume and duration. That commitment is the bankable revenue stream that
funds the commercial investment. The same model accelerated COVID vaccine development
by 18 months — Operation Warp Speed was advance purchase commitments for vaccines
that did not yet exist.*

### The International Framework Question

*TODO: The race dynamic. China, Luxembourg, the UAE, Japan, and India all have active

comparable to the advantages the United States gained from establishing the GPS standard

will not stay open indefinitely.*

## Tone Notes

This article must walk a careful line between genuine excitement and credibility. Space
mining attracts both serious commercial investors and breathless hype. The article should
be firmly in the former camp: specific about what exists, honest about timelines, clear
about what the government's role is and is not. The Operation Warp Speed comparison is
the most useful analogy — most readers understand that advance purchase commitments
accelerated vaccine development without the government "doing" the science. The same
mechanism applies here.
