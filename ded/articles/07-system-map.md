## Central Argument

Each program in this series is defensible on its own terms. But the strongest argument
for the full set is not that each program works individually — it is that they are
designed to reinforce each other. The Automation Tax Credit funds the Negative Income
Tax. The Negative Income Tax keeps displaced workers solvent while retraining. Retraining
moves workers into strategic sectors. Strategic sectors generate the productivity that
increases Automation Tax Credit revenue. The Automation Tax Credit funds the next round.
This is not a collection of programs. It is a machine — and machines that are designed
correctly generate more output than the sum of their parts.

## Key Evidence and Numbers to Build From

- The three core cycles: Productivity-to-Ownership (automation → Automation Tax Credit
  → income floor + training + American Sovereign Fund), Defense-to-Capital (arms sales
  → American Sovereign Fund → domestic capacity → cheaper arms → more sales), and
  Trade-to-Capacity (tariffs → American Sovereign Fund → domestic alternatives →
  competitive industry → tariffs can be reduced)
- Year 30 projection: approximately $190–540 billion per year in system inflow;
  approximately $470–570 billion per year in total citizen benefit (income support,
  healthcare coverage, training, dividends)
- The Build-Own-Benefit Loop: Civilian Reserve workers build the factories and facilities
  that the American Sovereign Fund Strategic Reserve owns; those assets generate the
  returns that fund the dividend those same workers receive as citizens. Labor builds
  capital; capital generates income; income flows back to labor.
- Self-funding trajectory: the system reaches fiscal self-sufficiency at approximately
  Year 25, when Automation Tax Credit revenue fully covers the net Negative Income Tax cost

## Tone Notes

This is the most technically ambitious article in the series. The challenge is making
system dynamics legible to a general reader. Use concrete examples for every loop —
never describe a feedback cycle in the abstract; always show it with specific numbers
and specific people. The Build-Own-Benefit Loop is the most emotionally powerful
element: the same person who builds the factory receives the dividend from it.
That is a different economy than the one we have.
