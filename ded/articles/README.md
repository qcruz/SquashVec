# DED Public Articles

This directory contains public-facing articles for Substack or blog publication.
Each article is a standalone piece that makes one proposal or concept accessible
to a general audience. Policy depth lives in the `ded/proposal/`, `ded/tools/`,
and `ded/economics/` directories. These articles are the public translation layer.

## Voice and Style Notes

These articles are written for a general audience — educated, curious, not specialists.
They should read like the best Substack writing: substantive enough to be taken seriously,
concrete enough to be remembered, and honest about what is uncertain.

**What these articles are:**
- A genuine argument, made with evidence, for a specific set of proposals
- Written in first-person plural where appropriate ("we" = Americans collectively)
- Willing to acknowledge costs, tradeoffs, and failure modes — because arguments that
  pretend problems don't exist are less persuasive, not more
- Data-backed but not data-dense: one or two numbers per claim, chosen for impact

**What these articles are not:**
- Policy documents — the depth lives in the source files
- Neutral explainers — these are arguments, not Wikipedia entries
- Promotional material — the goal is to persuade through reasoning, not enthusiasm

## Publication Series Concept

The ten articles are designed to work as a series, publishable in sequence, each
building on the last:

1. Start with the care economy (most emotionally accessible, universal constituency)
2. Move to ownership and the sovereign fund (the "what you'd receive" argument)
3. Then to the institutional case (why a new agency is the right answer)
4. Then to the philosophical foundation (economic citizenship as the frame)
5. Then to the technical proposals (supply chain, workforce, food, space)
6. Conclude with AI and automation (the most urgent near-term driver)

A reader who follows the series moves from "I recognize this problem" → "I see a solution"
→ "I understand why this institution is the right one" → "I believe in the framework"
→ "I want the specific programs" → "I understand why it's urgent now."
