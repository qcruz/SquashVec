## Why This Asset Scores 10

DARPA is the only federal institution purpose-built to fund technically risky, strategically
transformative R&D that the private sector will not fund at the required timeline. Its track
record is unmatched in the federal government:

**Technologies DARPA created or decisively accelerated:**
- **Internet (ARPANET, 1969):** Packet-switched networking; the architecture underlying all
  modern communications
- **GPS (1970s–80s):** Navigation technology now embedded in every smartphone, logistics
  system, missile, and autonomous vehicle
- **Stealth aircraft (1975):** Low-observable technology enabling F-117 and B-2
- **Autonomous vehicles (2004–07 Grand Challenge):** Triggered the modern self-driving car
  industry; every major AV program traces to DARPA Grand Challenge participants
- **mRNA vaccine platform foundation:** DARPA's ADEPT program funded early mRNA work that
  enabled the COVID vaccine platform ($25M DARPA investment → $10B+ COVID vaccine output)
- **Modern AI/machine learning infrastructure:** DARPA funded key early neural network,
  natural language, and computer vision research
- **Precision-guided munitions:** Smart bombs; completely transformed modern warfare economics

**The DARPA model's strategic value to DED:**

DARPA's funding model produces outcomes that are impossible through normal government R&D
procurement because:

1. **Program managers have full budget authority** — no committee approval required for
   technical pivots; decisions made in days, not months
2. **Failure is expected and acceptable** — 85–90% of programs fail by commercial standards;
   the 10–15% that succeed more than justify the portfolio
3. **Temporary rotating experts** — PMs come from the best institutions in the world for 4–5
   years and return; no institutional capture by career bureaucracy
4. **No internal labs** — all work contracted to universities, companies, and national labs;
   DARPA funds but does not execute
5. **"Heilmeier Catechism"** — every program must answer: What are you trying to do? How is
   it done today? What's new? What does success look like? What will it cost?

This model produces transformative outcomes that slower agencies cannot — which is why the DED
Accelerator program design explicitly models DARPA program structure.

## The DARPA Model as DED Template

The DED Accelerator program — documented in `tools/expert-reserve-program.md` — is explicitly
modeled on DARPA's organizational design. The key structural parallels:

| Element | DARPA | DED Accelerator |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Program authority | PM has full budget authority | Accelerator program director has full contract authority |
| Staff model | Rotating experts, 4–5 year terms | ERP experts, rotating assignments |
| Risk tolerance | Explicit; 85–90% failure rate acceptable | Explicit; outcomes measured at portfolio level |
| Contractor model | All work external; DARPA funds, doesn't execute | All contracts to private companies / national labs |
| Success metric | Technical milestone achievement | Phase transition criteria defined in advance |
| IP framework | Government license retained; contractor commercializes | Type D contract IP structure: DED royalty-free license, company retains commercial rights |

**Where the DED Accelerator extends beyond DARPA:**
- DARPA's mandate is defense; DED's mandate is economic resilience across all sectors
- DARPA does not do advance purchase commitments; DED combines R&D investment with APC anchor buying
- DARPA programs end at demonstration; DED contracts include scale-up provisions and IP licensing
  into the Asset Register
- DARPA's $4.5B/year budget covers the full mission; DED Accelerator programs are funded at
  scenario-specific rates ($500M–3B/program/year) with explicit scenario-linkage requirements

## DARPA's DED-Relevant Active Programs (2026)

### Electronics Resurgence Initiative (ERI)
- Budget: ~$1.5B cumulative
- Focus: Beyond-CMOS computing, chiplet architectures, new semiconductor materials (GaN, SiC),
  design automation
- DED linkage: Semiconductor Collapse scenario — domestic architecture diversity reduces
  concentration on TSMC's specific process nodes; chiplet standards reduce allied compatibility
  barriers

### ADEPT / Pandemic Prevention Platform (P3)
- Focus: Platform-based therapeutics (nucleic acid, antibody) deployable in 60 days vs. 12+ months
- DED linkage: Pandemic scenario — the 60-day platform concept is exactly the Phase 1 medical
  countermeasure deployment model DED is designed to support with APC commitments

### RADICS (Rapid Attack Detection, Isolation and Characterization Systems)
- Focus: Automated grid cyberattack detection and isolation; restore a degraded grid segment
  in 7 days (vs. current weeks-to-months)
- DED linkage: Grid Cyberattack scenario — RADICS capabilities directly inform DED air-gapped
  control system investment and recovery protocol design

### Biological Technologies Office (BTO) — Reforestation, Soil Carbon
- Focus: Engineered biological solutions for climate (carbon sequestration, drought tolerance)
- DED linkage: Drought scenario — drought-tolerant crop platforms; soil carbon programs that
  restore water retention in depleted agricultural soils

### GIDDEON (Generating Innovative Global Disruption Resistant Materials)
- Focus: Alternatives to rare earth elements in permanent magnets, sensors, and structural materials
- DED linkage: Rare Earth Denial scenario — rare-earth-free motor and magnet technology is
  exactly the substitution research the DED Accelerator funds in Phase 2

## DED Leverage Points

### DARPA as Co-Investor (Not Duplicated)

DED does not replicate DARPA. The correct DED role is:
1. **Bridge DARPA to commercial scale** — DARPA programs routinely end at TRL 4–6 (lab
   demonstration). DED Type D Accelerator contracts pick up at TRL 6 and fund scale-up to
   TRL 8–9 (commercial deployment). This is the gap DARPA explicitly does not fill.
2. **Add APC commitment to DARPA-developed technologies** — DARPA creates the technology;
   DED commits to purchase the output, completing the commercial case that private investors
   will not make for strategically critical but commercially uncertain products.
3. **IP licensing of DARPA-origin technology** — Some DARPA IP enters the federal IP estate
   under the Bayh-Dole Act. DED's IP Asset Register and licensing framework can systematically
   exploit this inventory for trade negotiation and allied technology sharing.

### DARPA Program Manager Alumni as ERP Candidates

Former DARPA program managers represent some of the most technically capable and
strategically sophisticated individuals in the federal talent pool. DED's Expert Reserve
Program should specifically recruit DARPA PM alumni for:
- Accelerator program director roles
- NEID technical advisory positions
- Scenario technical review panels (ensuring scenario response plans are technically sound)

## Vulnerabilities

### Budget Fragility
DARPA's $4.5B budget is small relative to its strategic impact — and is perennially subject
to congressional pressure for "accountability" that would undermine the failure-tolerant model.
Any move toward requiring DARPA to demonstrate near-term commercial value would destroy the
institutional model that produces its results.

**DED recommendation:** DED mission explicitly protects DARPA's operational independence as a
national strategic asset. DED JCED oversight framework includes DARPA budget protection as a
baseline scenario resilience requirement.

### Program Manager Recruiting Constraints
DARPA's model depends on recruiting the best technical talent from universities and companies
for 4–5 year rotations. Competition from industry — particularly AI companies paying $500k+
compensation packages — is making PM recruitment increasingly difficult.

**DED recommendation:** DED ERP framework includes a DARPA-feeding track — DED contracts
create career pathways that make DARPA PM service more financially viable (deferred compensation,
loan forgiveness, re-entry contracts with DED partner companies).

### Classification Barriers
Some DARPA work is classified at levels that prevent commercial transfer. DED's mission
requires moving technology from government R&D to commercial deployment — classification
can obstruct this pipeline.

**DED recommendation:** DED General Counsel maintains a standing classified-to-commercial
technology transfer working group with DoD, DARPA, and IC; identifies pathways for
controlled declassification of commercially relevant technologies.

## The DARPA Precedent and DED

Every DED Accelerator program proposal (space mining, synthetic protein, semiconductor
lithography diversification, battery chemistry substitution, rare-earth-free magnets) follows
the pattern DARPA established:

1. **Identify the strategic need** that market forces will not address on the required timeline
2. **Fund technical programs** with program managers who have full authority and accept failure
3. **Define success criteria** in advance (Heilmeier Catechism)
4. **Transfer the output** to commercial deployment (via APC, anchor buying, or IP licensing)
5. **Measure at portfolio level** — individual program failure is acceptable; portfolio
   outcome is the metric

DARPA is the proof that the U.S. government can successfully execute high-risk, high-reward
strategic R&D when the right organizational design is used. The DED Accelerator is the
economic security application of the same organizational design.
