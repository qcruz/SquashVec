# Strategic Asset Profile: U.S. Corn Belt

**Asset Category:** Physical Resources / Agricultural Production
**DED Score:** 9/10 (Criticality 10, Vulnerability 7, Substitutability 3, DED Leverage 8)
**Tier:** S
**Last Updated:** 2026-05

---

## Profile Overview

The U.S. Corn Belt — approximately 125 million acres of cropland across Iowa, Illinois,
Indiana, Ohio, Minnesota, South Dakota, Nebraska, Kansas, and Missouri — is the
highest-output agricultural region in the world. It produces approximately:

- 35–40% of global corn supply
- 35% of global soybean supply
- Significant shares of wheat, sorghum, and other feed grains

The Corn Belt is not merely a food production asset. It is the feedstock for U.S.
ethanol production (40% of corn goes to biofuels), a primary input to animal protein
production (cattle, pork, poultry feed chains), and an export commodity that anchors
U.S. trade balances with dozens of nations.

DED Score 9: Extremely high criticality (food security, energy feedstock, trade balance);
significant climate vulnerability (drought, heat stress, flooding); very low substitutability
(no global alternative at this scale); high DED leverage (Type A/B contracts, crop transition
payments, inverse yield bonds all directly applicable).

- Primary threat: sustained drought (2012 drought destroyed $35B in production in a single year)

  pest/disease (soybean rust, corn rootworm), input price shock (2022 fertilizer price spike)

  reduces crop diversity and amplifies single-event loss
