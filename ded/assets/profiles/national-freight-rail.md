# DED Asset Profile: National Freight Rail Network

**Asset Class:** Physical Infrastructure / Transportation
**DED Tier:** A (High Strategic Value)
**DED Score (estimated):** 7.8/10
**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Related Assets:** Mississippi River System, Interstate Highway System, SPR pipeline network
**Relevant Scenarios:** Drought, Shipping Blockade, Oil Restriction, Pandemic, Rare Earth Denial

---

## Overview

The U.S. Class I freight rail network (7 railroads; ~140,000 route miles; ~$80B/year freight revenue)
is the single highest-capacity, lowest-cost freight mode for bulk commodities — grain, coal, crude
oil, chemicals, intermodal containers. Under any DED scenario that disrupts trucking (fuel shortage,
driver shortage) or marine shipping (blockade, river closure), rail becomes the emergency backbone
for the entire domestic supply chain. The network's strategic value is severely underestimated in
peacetime because it is never close to capacity — under crisis conditions, that latent capacity
becomes the difference between functional and collapsed domestic supply chains.

Despite this value, the freight rail network carries significant concentration risks: the Class I
network is private, highly consolidated (7 railroads control 90%+ of traffic), and has
systematically reduced buffer capacity (railcars, locomotive fleets, crew reserves) under
precision scheduled railroading (PSR) efficiency programs. DED analysis must address both
the network's strategic value and the PSR-induced fragility.
