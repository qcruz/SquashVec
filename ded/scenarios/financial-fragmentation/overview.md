# Scenario: Global Financial System Fragmentation

## Scenario Definition

A breakdown of the integrated global financial system — through dollar weaponization
backlash, SWIFT exclusions, competing payment systems, sovereign debt crises, or
active financial warfare — that disrupts cross-border payments, trade finance,
and currency stability for major trading partners.

---

## Why This Is Different

Financial disruption does not destroy physical goods. It destroys the **coordination
mechanism** for obtaining them. Countries that cannot pay for imports or cannot
receive payment for exports stop trading — even if the physical goods exist.

---

## Severity Tiers

| Tier | Description | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Specific country/sector exclusions from SWIFT | Russia 2022 |
| Tier 2 | Dollar alternative bloc (BRICS+ currency) gains 20%+ trade share | Emerging trend |
| Tier 3 | Global dollar crisis; trade finance breakdown | Hypothetical |

---

## Phase Responses

### Phase 1: Payment Continuity

- **Allied payment backstop:** Pre-negotiated bilateral currency swap lines with key trading partners
- **Commodity-backed payment alternatives:** Pre-designed frameworks for barter/commodity trade
- **Trade finance reserve:** Treasury facility to backstop critical import finance

### Phase 2: Resilience Building

- **Domestic production of financial system inputs:** Reduce reliance on financial access for critical goods
- **Diversified reserve currency exposure:** Reduce single-currency concentration in foreign reserves
- **Digital dollar infrastructure:** CBDC interoperability with allied payment systems

### Phase 3: Structural

- **Export diversification:** Reduce reliance on any single market for exports
- **Import substitution for highest-risk categories:** Identify goods most vulnerable to payment disruption

---

## DED Programs Summary

Programs proposed across all phases of this scenario and how they reduce impact:

**Pre-Positioned Financial Infrastructure**
- **Allied currency swap lines** — pre-negotiated bilateral swap agreements with key trading partners (Japan, EU, UK, Canada, South Korea, Australia); activated immediately on Tier 1 declaration; ensures payment continuity for the highest-volume trade relationships without relying on SWIFT or dollar clearing
- **Trade finance reserve facility** — Treasury backstop facility for critical import finance when commercial trade finance seizes; pre-authorized on declaration; prevents the physical goods shortage that follows payment system breakdown

**Phase 1 — Payment Continuity**
- **TRAL deployment in trade-disrupted sectors** — tax return advances for workers in import-dependent industries facing layoffs due to supply chain breakdown from payment disruption; maintains household income without requiring new appropriation
- **Commodity-backed payment frameworks** — pre-designed bilateral barter and commodity trade agreements for the most critical goods (energy, food, pharmaceutical inputs) with major trading partners; activated when dollar-denominated trade breaks down
- **Type A contracts with critical importers** — pre-committed supply agreements with domestic distributors for the highest-priority imported goods; ensures they maintain inventory through the disruption period

**Phase 2 — Resilience Building**
- **Import substitution contracts (Type C)** — DED accelerates domestic production of goods most vulnerable to payment disruption; reduces the consequence of dollar exclusion by reducing import dependence
- **Digital dollar / CBDC infrastructure investment (Type B)** — DED co-investment in interoperable payment infrastructure with allied nations; reduces dependence on SWIFT for allied trade without creating a fragmentation-accelerating alternative
- **IP licensing as financial diplomacy** — DED uses government IP licensing concessions to strengthen bilateral trade relationships with nations considering dollar alternatives; converts the IP trade tool into a financial system stability instrument

**Long-Run**
- **Strategic import reduction program** — systematic DED analysis of which import categories can be domestically sourced at acceptable cost; targeted Type B contracts to build that domestic capacity before payment fragmentation makes imports impossible
