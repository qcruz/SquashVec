# Scenario: Global Financial System Fragmentation

## Scenario Definition

A breakdown of the integrated global financial system — through dollar weaponization
backlash, SWIFT exclusions, competing payment systems, sovereign debt crises, or
active financial warfare — that disrupts cross-border payments, trade finance,
and currency stability for major trading partners.

---

## Why This Is Different

Financial disruption does not destroy physical goods. It destroys the **coordination
mechanism** for obtaining them. Countries that cannot pay for imports or cannot
receive payment for exports stop trading — even if the physical goods exist.

---

## Severity Tiers

| Tier | Description | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Specific country/sector exclusions from SWIFT | Russia 2022 |
| Tier 2 | Dollar alternative bloc (BRICS+ currency) gains 20%+ trade share | Emerging trend |
| Tier 3 | Global dollar crisis; trade finance breakdown | Hypothetical |

---

## Phase Responses

### Phase 1: Payment Continuity

- **Allied payment backstop:** Pre-negotiated bilateral currency swap lines with key trading partners
- **Commodity-backed payment alternatives:** Pre-designed frameworks for barter/commodity trade
- **Trade finance reserve:** Treasury facility to backstop critical import finance

### Phase 2: Resilience Building

- **Domestic production of financial system inputs:** Reduce reliance on financial access for critical goods
- **Diversified reserve currency exposure:** Reduce single-currency concentration in foreign reserves
- **Digital dollar infrastructure:** CBDC interoperability with allied payment systems

### Phase 3: Structural

- **Export diversification:** Reduce reliance on any single market for exports
- **Import substitution for highest-risk categories:** Identify goods most vulnerable to payment disruption
