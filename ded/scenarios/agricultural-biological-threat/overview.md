# Scenario: Agricultural Biological Threat

**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Status:** New scenario category — identified via RT-003 (Varroa-X) red team exercise

---

## Scenario Definition

A rapidly spreading biological pathogen — crop disease, livestock disease, or pollinator
pathogen — that eliminates or severely reduces output from a major component of the U.S.
agricultural system within 1–3 growing seasons, before effective treatment or containment
is available.

This category is distinct from drought (water-driven) and oil restriction (input cost-driven)
agricultural disruptions. Biological threats spread exponentially, are hard to contain once
established, are often initially misidentified as routine crop/livestock stress, and may
have no available treatment at the time of peak economic damage.

---

## Why This Is a Distinct Scenario

Agricultural biological threats share structural features with pandemic (exponential spread,
treatment development timeline) but target the food supply rather than human health.
They share economic consequences with drought (crop failure, price spikes, rural income
collapse) but through a different mechanism with different response instruments.

The combination of features makes this a scenario the DED cannot address purely with
instruments from existing categories — it requires its own phase structure, early warning
systems, and pre-positioned instruments.

## Candidate Triggering Events (to be researched)

*TODO: Assess probability and impact for each*

- **Wheat stem rust Ug99 and variants:** Already present in Africa/Middle East; resistant to
  most deployed resistance genes; one North American introduction could destroy 60–80% of
  wheat crop before new varieties are available
- **Citrus greening (HLB) national spread:** Currently limited to Florida and Texas; if it
  reaches California citrus, eliminates $3.4B citrus industry with no cure available
- **Avian influenza H5N1 → sustained poultry-to-poultry transmission:** 2022–24 outbreak
  already destroyed 100M+ birds; a more transmissible strain could destroy the entire
  commercial poultry system within one season
- **Varroa-X style pollinator collapse:** Analyzed in RT-003; treatment-resistant Varroa
  biotype as archetype for this scenario category
- **Soybean rust (Phakopsora pachyrhizi) established in Midwest:** Already in the South;
  if it reaches the Corn Belt, estimated 10–70% yield loss without fungicide protection;
  and resistance is developing to all major fungicide classes

## Key DED Programs (to be designed)

*TODO: Full phase file development*

- **NEID Agricultural Biological Threat Index** — Real-time monitoring of pathogen spread,
  treatment resistance reports, and crop health indicators (USDA NASS, APHIS, CDC animal health)
- **Industry Infrastructure Reserve Contracts** — Beekeeping, poultry, livestock: Type A
  contracts preserving minimum viable production infrastructure during crisis
- **Emergency Treatment R&D Accelerator** — Type D contracts for accelerated acaricide,
  fungicide, antiviral, and resistance breeding programs; USDA-ARS + university + commercial
- **Crop Diversification Transition Payments** — Managed transition out of threatened crops in
  high-risk zones; prevents chaotic land value collapse
- **Alternative Source Development** — Allied sourcing Type C contracts for threatened crop
  categories; pre-negotiated before crisis
