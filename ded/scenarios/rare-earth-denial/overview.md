# Scenario: Rare Earth and Critical Mineral Supply Denial

## Scenario Definition

A deliberate or accidental disruption to the supply of rare earth elements (REEs)
and critical minerals — through Chinese export controls, geopolitical conflict,
mine collapse, or coordinated denial — that impairs U.S. manufacturing of
defense systems, clean energy technology, electronics, and industrial equipment.

---

## Why This Is a Structural Vulnerability

China controls an extraordinary share of critical mineral supply chains:

| Mineral | Chinese Share of Production | Chinese Share of Processing | Key Uses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rare earth elements | ~60% mining | ~85% processing | Magnets, defense, electronics |
| Cobalt (via DRC) | Controls refining | ~80% | EV batteries, aerospace |
| Lithium (via processing) | ~60% refining | — | EV batteries, storage |
| Gallium | ~80% production | ~98% | Semiconductors, LEDs |
| Germanium | ~60% production | ~95% | Fiber optics, semiconductors |
| Graphite (anode) | ~90% production | — | EV batteries |
| Tungsten | ~80% production | — | Defense, cutting tools |
| Magnesium | ~85% production | — | Aluminum alloys, aerospace |

China has already demonstrated willingness to use mineral exports as geopolitical
leverage (2010 Japan rare earth embargo, 2023 gallium/germanium export controls).

---

## Severity Tiers

| Tier | Disruption | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Controls on 1–3 mineral categories | Export licensing restrictions (2023 pattern) |
| Tier 2 | Controls on rare earths + battery minerals | Escalated Taiwan tensions |
| Tier 3 | Full denial of all Chinese-controlled minerals | Active conflict scenario |

---

## Critical Downstream Effects

**Defense:** Rare earth permanent magnets are in every guided missile, fighter jet
engine, radar system, submarine motor, and satellite. No domestic substitute at scale.

**EV transition:** Cobalt, lithium, and graphite are in every EV battery.
A rare earth denial during a transition away from oil is particularly damaging —
the substitute depends on the same disrupted supply.

**Electronics and grid:** Gallium, germanium, and indium are in semiconductors,
solar panels, and power electronics. The clean energy transition requires more of them.

---

## Phase Responses

### Phase 1: Inventory and Triage

- **National critical mineral inventory:** Classify all mineral dependencies by security criticality
- **Strategic stockpile activation:** DoD maintains some REE stockpiles; expand to civilian uses
- **Allied sourcing:** Australia, Canada, Greenland, Brazil — accelerate extraction agreements
- **Recycling activation:** Urban mining from electronics scrap; currently minimal but scalable

### Phase 2: Supply Diversification

- **MP Materials and domestic REE expansion:** Fast-track permitting for Mountain Pass and new sites
- **Allied processing capacity:** Build REE processing outside China (currently almost none)
- **Battery chemistry diversification:** R&D and contracts for lithium-iron-phosphate (no cobalt), sodium-ion (no lithium)
- **Substitution research:** Magnets without rare earths (ferrite, alternative formulations)

### Phase 3: Structural Independence

- **Domestic rare earth processing:** End-to-end from mining to magnet manufacturing in the U.S.
- **Material efficiency standards:** Require reduced rare earth content in government-procured equipment
- **International mineral reserve:** Coordinate with G7 on shared strategic stockpiles
- **Circular economy mandate:** Recovery rates for critical minerals from end-of-life products
