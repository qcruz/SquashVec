# Semiconductor Collapse — Phase 3: Long-Run Resilience

**Scenario:** Semiconductor Supply Collapse
**Phase:** 3 of 3
**Timeline:** 7–15 years from declaration
**Trigger:** Phase 2 transition criteria met (see phase-2-domestic-production.md)
**Last Updated:** 2026-05

---

## Phase 3 Objective

Phase 3 represents the post-transition semiconductor economy: U.S. and allied domestic
production has reached 20%+ of advanced chip consumption; the strategic chip reserve is
fully operational; the workforce pipeline is self-sustaining; and alternative lithography
research has reached pre-production scale. DED's role shifts from crisis investment to
long-run resilience maintenance — ensuring the gains are not lost to the same market
forces that created the concentration in the first place.

## Key Programs (to be built out)

### 1. Concentration Monitoring and Anti-Backslide Standards

*TODO: Full design*

- The market will always tend toward concentration in semiconductor manufacturing — the
  economics favor scale and colocation. Phase 3 requires permanent monitoring to prevent
  the domestic and allied capacity built in Phase 2 from consolidating back into single
  points of failure.
- NEID develops a Semiconductor Concentration Index (SCI): measures production concentration
  by node class, geography, and company annually. JCED review triggered if SCI exceeds
  Phase 2 entry thresholds.
- DED contract terms include anti-consolidation provisions: Type A contracts lapse if a
  contracting company is acquired by a foreign entity without DED approval.

### 2. Post-EUV Lithography Commercialization

- Phase 2's alternative lithography R&D (MBEBL, DSA, NIL) should reach production-relevant

- Breaking the ASML monopoly is a Phase 3 objective, not a Phase 2 objective. Phase 3 is
  when the research investment converts to commercial capability.
- U.S.-based alternatives would represent a major shift in semiconductor geopolitics —
  and a DED IP Asset Register entry of significant diplomatic value.
