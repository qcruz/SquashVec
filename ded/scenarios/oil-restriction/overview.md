# Scenario: Extended Global Oil Restriction

## Scenario Definition

A sustained, large-scale reduction in global oil supply — caused by geopolitical
conflict, coordinated embargo, major producer collapse, or naval blockade of
critical shipping routes — that persists for months to years and cannot be offset
by existing reserves or alternative sources within the short term.

Oil is not simply a fuel. It is a **platform input** for the modern economy:
- Trucking, freight, aviation, shipping
- Nitrogen fertilizer (natural gas, but tightly coupled to oil economics)
- Plastics, chemicals, pharmaceuticals
- Agricultural equipment operation
- Mining and raw material extraction
- Military readiness and logistics
- Emergency services
- Suburban geography (designed around personal vehicle access)

An oil restriction is therefore simultaneously:
a fuel crisis, a food crisis, a logistics crisis, an industrial crisis,
and a social stability crisis — on a single time axis.

---

## Severity Tiers

| Tier | Supply Reduction | Trigger Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | 10–25% | Regional conflict, one major exporter disrupted |
| **Tier 2** | 25–60% | OPEC+ embargo, Strait of Hormuz blockade |
| **Tier 3** | 60–100%+ | Global conflict, catastrophic producer collapse |

Plans below are written for **Tier 2** as the planning baseline.
Tier 1 uses a subset of Phase 1 tools. Tier 3 requires Phase 2 mobilization
beginning immediately rather than after six months.

---

## Cascade Timeline

| Week | Primary Effects | Secondary Effects |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Price spike, panic buying | Hoarding, spot shortages |
| 2–4 | Fuel rationing pressure | Freight slowdowns, airline reductions |
| 1–2 mo | Agricultural fuel crunch | Planting/harvest disruption risk |
| 2–3 mo | Industrial slowdowns | Manufacturing output drops |
| 3–6 mo | Food price inflation | Social unrest pressure |
| 6+ mo | Structural adaptation required | Political pressure, reserve depletion |
