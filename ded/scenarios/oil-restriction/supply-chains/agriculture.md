# Oil Restriction — Agriculture Supply Chain

## Why Agriculture Is a Priority-1 Dependency

An oil crisis becomes a food crisis faster than most people expect.
The modern food system depends on oil at every stage:

| Stage | Oil Dependency |
|---|---|
| Fertilizer production | Natural gas (co-priced with oil) → ammonia → nitrogen fertilizer |
| Field operations | Diesel for tractors, combines, irrigation pumps |
| Crop drying | Propane and natural gas |
| Food processing | Diesel and electricity (grid often gas-powered) |
| Cold chain | Diesel refrigeration in trucks and warehouses |
| Retail distribution | Last-mile trucking |

**Time-to-crisis:** A disruption that begins at planting season and reduces
fertilizer availability by 30% can reduce crop yields by 20–40% — with effects
felt 6–12 months later at harvest.

## Critical Node: Nitrogen Fertilizer

The most acute agricultural vulnerability in an oil/gas disruption.

**Dependency chain:**
```
Natural gas → Haber-Bosch synthesis → Ammonia → Urea/ammonium nitrate → Crop yield
```

**U.S. nitrogen fertilizer situation:**
- ~40% imported (primarily from Canada, Russia, Trinidad)
- Domestic production concentrated at ~20 major facilities
- Production is natural-gas-intensive (~33 MMBtu per ton of ammonia)

**Substitution options:**

| Substitute | Lead Time | Scale Potential |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic reserve (6–18 months supply) | Pre-built | High (immediate) |
| Domestic production expansion | 2–4 years | Moderate |
| Organic nitrogen (manure, compost) | 1–2 seasons | Limited at scale |
| Precision application (reduce waste) | Immediate | 10–20% efficiency gain |
| Crop rotation (legume nitrogen-fixing) | 1 season | Moderate, yield tradeoff |

**Pre-built requirement:** Strategic fertilizer reserve of 6–18 months
of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium for domestic agriculture.

## Crop Prioritization Framework

Under resource constraints, crop mix matters.

**Calories per acre (illustrative):**

| Crop | Cal/acre/year | Oil/water intensity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Potatoes | ~15M | Low | High calorie density |
| Corn (food) | ~12M | High | Fertilizer-intensive |
| Wheat | ~7M | Medium | Staple, storable |
| Soybeans | ~5M | Medium | Protein, oil |
| Beef (feedlot) | ~1M | Very high | Inefficient conversion |
| Corn (ethanol) | ~4M equiv. | Very high | Competes with food |

**Emergency priority shift:**
- Reduce biofuel crop allocation (corn ethanol uses ~40% of U.S. corn crop)
- Reduce feedlot beef in favor of direct-consumption calories
- Increase potato, wheat, and legume production
- Reduce water-intensive export crops in drought-vulnerable regions
