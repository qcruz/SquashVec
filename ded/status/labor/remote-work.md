# Status: Labor — Remote Work Capacity

**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Update Frequency:** Quarterly
**Relevant Scenarios:** Pandemic, Oil Restriction

---

## Current Remote Work Baseline (2025–2026)

### Workforce Share Working Remotely

| Mode | Share of Workforce | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| Fully remote | ~13% | Down from 2020 peak (~35%); stabilized above pre-pandemic level |
| Hybrid (≥1 day/week remote) | ~28% | Largest growing segment |
| Fully in-person | ~59% | Includes all non-remote-capable roles |
| Remote-capable but currently in-person | ~15% (est.) | Could shift to remote under mandate |

**Total remote-capable workforce estimate:** ~40-45% of all U.S. workers could work fully or partially remotely given adequate infrastructure and employer policy.

## Employer Remote Work Preparedness

| Factor | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Employers with formal remote work policy | ~45% (est.) |
| Employers with tested business continuity plans | ~20% (est.) |
| Employers who deployed remote work in 2020 | ~35% |
| VPN and collaboration tool penetration (enterprise) | High (>80% of white-collar employers) |
| Mandated employer remote work plans | None — no federal requirement |

**Key gap:** There is no federal requirement for employers to maintain or test remote work capability. Most plans from 2020 have atrophied. A sudden mandate would face significant implementation delays at the employer level.

## Emergency Remote Work Mobilization Capacity

Under a declared pandemic emergency with remote work mandates:

**Mobilizable within 2 weeks:**
- Tech, finance, professional services with existing infrastructure: ~15-20M workers
- Government agencies (federal + large state): ~5-8M workers
- **Subtotal: ~20-28M workers**

**Mobilizable within 4-8 weeks (requires employer preparation):**
- Remaining remote-capable private sector with hardware/software procurement: ~20-30M workers
- Schools (full transition to virtual learning): ~55M students, ~3M teachers
- **Subtotal: additional ~25-35M workers**

**Cannot mobilize remotely (inherently in-person):**
- Food processing, manufacturing, agriculture, construction, healthcare delivery, utilities, transportation: ~80-90M workers
- These workers require the Phase 1 in-person protection framework

---

## Key Gaps and Risks

1. **Rural broadband:** ~6-8M households lack adequate broadband; pandemic remote work mandate would leave rural workers unable to comply
2. **No employer continuity mandate:** Employers have no legal obligation to maintain remote work capability; readiness has atrophied since 2020
3. **Device equity:** ~10-15M U.S. households lack a computer or tablet; smartphone-only households cannot perform most office work remotely
4. **School remote learning infrastructure:** K-12 remote learning capacity deteriorated post-2020; many districts have reduced device fleets and IT staffing
5. **Childcare-remote work conflict:** Remote work does not solve the childcare problem — parents cannot work from home while supervising young children

| Gap | Status | Priority |
