# Status: EV Adoption and Charging Infrastructure

**Last updated:** 2026-05  
**Next review:** 2026-08  
**Sources:** IEA, EIA, BloombergNEF, AFDC (DOE)

## U.S. EV Market Share

| Metric | Value | Date |
|---|---|---|
| New EV sales share (all electric) | ~8.5% | 2025 |
| New EV + PHEV sales share | ~12% | 2025 |
| Total EVs on U.S. roads | ~4.5 million | 2025 |
| Total light vehicles on U.S. roads | ~290 million | 2025 |
| EV share of total fleet | ~1.5% | 2025 |

> **Key implication for DED:** Even at accelerated adoption, EVs are a
> small fraction of the total fleet. Oil displacement from current EV fleet
> is ~1–2% of personal vehicle fuel consumption. Fleet transition is slow.

## Fleet Electrification Progress

| Fleet | EV Share | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| USPS delivery fleet | ~12% | Long-term contract with Oshkosh underway |
| Transit buses | ~6% | Growing, federal funding available |
| School buses | ~3% | Early stage |
| Ride-share (Uber/Lyft) | ~8% | Urban concentration |
| Long-haul trucking | <1% | Range and charging are major constraints |

## Emergency Mobilization Baseline

For DED Phase 2 planning purposes:
- Current EV manufacturing capacity: ~2 million units/year (U.S.)
- Battery cell domestic capacity: ~150 GWh/year (growing rapidly)
- Time to double manufacturing capacity under Defense Production Act: est. 2–4 years
