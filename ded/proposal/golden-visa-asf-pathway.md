# DED Proposal: American Prosperity Visa (APV) — Golden Visa Pathway to ASF Eligibility

**Document Type:** Immigration and investment program proposal
**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Category:** Immigration / American Sovereign Fund / Capital Formation
**DED Priority:** Medium-High — accelerates ASF capital formation; creates a merit-based
  immigration pathway tied to long-run national wealth building; generates a self-selecting
  high-capital immigrant cohort with strong integration incentives

## 1. The Core Concept

The American Prosperity Visa is a new immigrant investor pathway that:

1. **Qualifies the investor** for accelerated American Sovereign Fund dividend eligibility
   by treating years of visa status as accruing toward the 18-year citizenship dividend
   clock — creating an explicit long-run financial stake in American economic success
   from the moment of arrival

2. **Automatically qualifies American-born children** of APV holders for the full
   18-year citizenship benefit accrual clock starting at birth — providing a generational
   integration incentive that no existing visa program offers

3. **Funds the American Sovereign Fund directly** through a mandatory ASF contribution
   that is distinct from — and additional to — the investment required for visa qualification,
   ensuring that each visa issued grows the fund that pays dividends to all eligible Americans

4. **Competes globally** with Portugal's Golden Visa, UAE's Golden Visa, Singapore's
   Global Investor Programme, and the EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program by offering something
   no competitor can: a share in a growing national wealth fund whose dividend compounds
   over decades

## 2. The Global Golden Visa Market

The United States is currently losing high-net-worth immigrants to competitor programs.
This is not a theoretical concern — it is documented capital and human capital flight.

### Competing Programs

| Country | Program | Minimum Investment | Pathway to Citizenship | Dividend/Return |
|---------|---------|-------------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Portugal | Golden Visa | €280k-€500k | 5 years → citizenship | None (residence access) |
| Spain | Golden Visa | €500k real estate | 10 years → citizenship | None |
| Greece | Golden Visa | €250k real estate | 7 years → citizenship | None |
| UAE | Golden Visa | AED 2M (~$545k) | Permanent residency (not citizenship) | None |
| Singapore | Global Investor Programme | SGD 2.5M (~$1.85M) | 2 years → permanent residence | None |
| Malta | Individual Investor Programme | €600k-€750k | 1-3 years → citizenship | None |
| U.S. EB-5 | Immigrant Investor | $800k-$1.05M + 10 jobs | 2 years → green card; 5 years → citizenship | None (regulatory) |

**The common failure of all existing programs:** They offer residency or citizenship as
a transactional exchange for investment. There is no ongoing financial relationship between
the investor and the country after the initial investment requirement is met.

**The APV structural advantage:** The American Sovereign Fund dividend creates a permanent,
growing financial relationship. An investor who arrives at age 40 and holds APV status
for 5 years before naturalization, then naturalizes and holds citizenship until the 18-year
dividend threshold (reached at age 63), begins receiving a growing annual dividend —
and can see the projected trajectory of that dividend from Day 1 of their visa application.

This is not residency as a product. It is ownership of a share in the American economy,
purchased through investment and immigration commitment, paying dividends for life.

### The U.S. EB-5 Problem

The existing EB-5 program has structural failures that the APV is designed to address:

**Job creation requirement is gameable:** EB-5 requires the creation or preservation of
10 full-time U.S. jobs per investor. Regional Center projects (the primary EB-5 investment
vehicle) have been plagued by fraud, project failures, and jobs that were counted but
never created. The SEC has brought over 50 enforcement actions against EB-5 regional
centers for fraud.

**No investor return:** EB-5 investors receive no financial return from their investment —
they are making a $800k-$1.05M investment in exchange for a visa, with the investment
returned (if successful) at below-market rate. There is no financial incentive for
high-quality immigrants to use EB-5 over competing programs that offer equivalent
immigration outcomes with lower investment requirements.

**Processing backlogs:** EB-5 visa processing takes 5-10 years for investors from
high-demand countries (China, India, Vietnam). High-net-worth investors who can choose
their destination do not wait 10 years for a visa.

## 3. Program Design

### 3.1 Eligibility and Investment Tiers

The APV operates on two tiers to capture both ultra-high-net-worth investors and
high-net-worth professionals whose human capital exceeds their investable capital:

**Tier 1 — Capital Investor**

*Requirement:*
- Net worth of ≥$3M (verified by financial institution or licensed auditor)
- Minimum direct ASF contribution: $500,000 (deposited directly into the ASF; not a loan,
  not a job-creation investment — a direct capital contribution to the national fund)
- Minimum U.S.-based investment: $1.5M in qualifying assets (U.S. equities, U.S. real
  estate outside of residential primary home, U.S. business equity, U.S. Treasury securities)
- Clean criminal record; national security screening (standard for investor visas)
- Source-of-funds documentation (FATF anti-money-laundering standards)

*Benefits:*
- Immediate 5-year renewable work and residency authorization (APV-1 status)
- APV years of status count toward ASF dividend eligibility clock at 1:1 ratio
  (5 years of APV status + 13 years of citizenship = 18-year threshold met)
- Expedited naturalization pathway: eligible for naturalization after 5 years of APV
  status without the standard 5-year green card holding period (total timeline:
  5 years APV → naturalization → 13 more years of citizenship → first dividend)
- American-born children of APV-1 holders: citizenship at birth; 18-year dividend
  clock starts at birth (natural-born citizens are eligible at age 18 regardless of
  parents' immigration status)
- Priority processing: 90-day processing target (vs. EB-5 5-10 year backlog)

**Tier 2 — Human Capital Investor**

For high-value professionals whose human capital and income trajectory exceed their
current investable assets — the immigrant entrepreneur, the accomplished scientist, the
senior executive relocating a business division:

*Requirement:*
- Annual income of ≥$500,000 in each of the prior 3 years (verified by tax returns
  from country of origin), OR
- Documented valuation of founded company of ≥$5M with active revenue, OR
- Minimum 10 years in a DED-designated critical skill category (advanced semiconductor
  engineering, quantum computing, AI safety research, biotechnology, advanced materials)
- Minimum direct ASF contribution: $100,000 (lower than Tier 1; reflecting that human
  capital, not financial capital, is the primary contribution)
- U.S.-based investment of $300,000 minimum OR a signed employment contract with a
  U.S. employer in a DED-priority sector at ≥$200,000/year salary
- Clean criminal record; national security screening

*Benefits:*
- 3-year renewable work and residency authorization (APV-2 status)
- APV years count toward ASF dividend clock at 0.5:1 ratio (5 years APV-2 = 2.5 years
  toward dividend clock; incentivizes naturalization to reach full accrual rate)
- Standard naturalization pathway: green card after 3 years APV-2 + 5 years green card
  = 8 years to naturalization; 18-year dividend clock begins at naturalization
- American-born children: same as Tier 1 — citizenship at birth, 18-year clock from birth
- DED skill registry recognition: APV-2 holders in DED-priority categories are
  automatically added to the Expert Reserve Program candidate database

### 3.2 The ASF Contribution Mechanism

The ASF contribution is the structural innovation that distinguishes the APV from all
competing programs. It is not an investment with a return expectation — it is a direct
capital contribution to the national fund, in exchange for which the contributor and
their descendants receive the rights that come with citizenship: the 18-year dividend
clock, accelerated in proportion to the contribution.

**How contributions flow:**
- APV applicant submits $500,000 (Tier 1) or $100,000 (Tier 2) to a designated U.S.
  Treasury account during the application process
- Funds are transferred to the ASF within 30 days of visa approval
- The contribution is invested per the ASF's standard investment mandate (75% Commercial
  Portfolio, 25% Strategic Reserve)
- The contributor receives no direct return on the contribution (it is not a loan or equity
  purchase) — the return is the accelerated dividend eligibility and the immigration pathway

**What the contribution is not:**
- Not a fee: fees go to administrative costs, not investment capital
- Not a loan: the contributor does not receive principal back
- Not equity in a specific company: it is invested in the broadly diversified ASF portfolio
- Not taxable income: it is a capital contribution to a government investment vehicle,
  treated as a non-deductible charitable contribution for U.S. tax purposes

**The long-run alignment:** A Tier 1 investor who contributes $500,000 to the ASF has a
permanent stake in the ASF's success. A larger, better-performing ASF means a larger
dividend for the investor's future eligible years. The investor's financial interest is
perfectly aligned with the fund's success — unlike the EB-5 model where the investor's
interest is solely in getting their principal back.

### 3.3 Annual Program Scale and ASF Capital Impact

**Volume projection:**

| Year | APV-1 Grants | APV-2 Grants | ASF Contributions |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 (ramp) | 1,000 | 3,000 | $600M ($500M + $300M) |
| 3 | 3,000 | 8,000 | $2.3B |
| 5 | 5,000 | 12,000 | $3.7B |
| 10 | 8,000 | 20,000 | $6.0B |

*Annual APV-1 cap: 10,000. Annual APV-2 cap: 25,000. Caps reviewed every 5 years.*

At Year 10, APV contributions add approximately $6B/year to ASF capital — roughly 10%
of the ASF's base annual contribution from arms sales, tariffs, and tax streams.
This is meaningful but not dominant; the APV's primary value is human capital and
integration incentive, not capital volume.

**Comparable reference:** The EB-5 program at peak generated approximately $3.5B/year
in investment (2015-2019 peak period). The APV, with a simpler structure, faster
processing, and a compelling long-run financial incentive, should exceed EB-5 peak
volume within 3-5 years of launch.

### 3.4 The Children's Eligibility Provision

This is the most politically powerful and strategically important element of the program.

American-born children of APV holders are U.S. citizens at birth (14th Amendment;
unconditional). Their 18-year dividend clock starts at birth — the same as any American-born
citizen. This means:

- An APV-1 investor who arrives at age 40 and has a child at age 42 → the child becomes
  eligible for the ASF dividend at age 18 (the investor's child is a full citizen at
  birth, begins accruing toward dividend eligibility immediately)
- The investor themselves, reaching the 18-year dividend threshold after naturalization,
  receives the dividend alongside their child — both are fully eligible American owners
  of the national fund

**Why this matters for immigration strategy:**
Most immigration systems treat the immigrant's children as secondary beneficiaries of
the parent's immigration status. The APV inverts this: the children's economic citizenship
is immediate and full from birth, regardless of the parent's naturalization timeline.
This creates an extraordinary integration incentive — the immigrant family's second
generation is fully embedded in the American economic ownership structure from Day 1.

For the ASF specifically: a 2028 baby born to an APV holder receives their first dividend
at age 18 in 2046, by which point the ASF has been compounding for 20 years and the
dividend may be $400-800/year. That child grows up as an owner of the American economy.

**The political framing:** This is not a special benefit for wealthy immigrants.
American-born citizens already receive citizenship at birth and the 18-year dividend
clock from birth. The APV provision does not create a new benefit — it ensures that
children of legal immigrant investors receive the same birthright that all American-born
citizens receive. Any argument that this is preferential treatment for immigrants is
an argument that American-born children of immigrants should have fewer rights than
other American-born citizens — a position that is constitutionally indefensible.

## 4. National Security Design

Investment visa programs are a known vector for foreign government influence, money
laundering, and espionage. The APV must be designed from the outset to resist these
failure modes.

### 4.1 Restricted Country List

APV applications from nationals of countries designated as Foreign Adversaries under
15 C.F.R. Part 7 (currently China, Russia, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Venezuela, and
the Maduro regime) are subject to enhanced review:

- APV-1 and APV-2 from restricted country nationals require CFIUS-equivalent review
  (Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States applies when the investment
  threshold is triggered; APV-level investment would trigger CFIUS in most cases)
- Tier 2 APV for restricted country nationals in DED-priority technology sectors
  (semiconductors, AI, quantum) requires IC clearance before approval
- Not an outright ban: legal immigration from these countries is a longstanding U.S.
  policy tool; but the level of scrutiny is proportionate to the risk

### 4.2 Source of Funds Verification

**Anti-money-laundering standards:** The APV contribution and qualifying investment
must pass FinCEN source-of-funds verification at the level applied to FinCEN's Customer
Due Diligence Rule (Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk profiles). An investment
manager or U.S. financial institution is required as an intermediary for all APV
investments — removing the ability to contribute cash or foreign-held assets directly.

**Beneficial ownership disclosure:** APV applicants must disclose all beneficial owners
of the assets being invested (no shell company structures without disclosed beneficial owners).
Treasury's Corporate Transparency Act infrastructure provides the legal and technical
framework for this requirement.

**Sanctions screening:** All APV applicants are screened against OFAC sanctions lists,
Interpol databases, and the DHS national security databases as part of standard
processing. This is not new; it is the standard investor visa screening enhanced with
CFIUS-adjacent review for the financial investment component.

### 4.3 No Fast-Track Security Clearances

APV status does not grant, accelerate, or presuppose any U.S. government security
clearance. Naturalized APV holders are eligible for security clearance through the
standard process — no different from any other naturalized citizen. The program does
not create a path to clearance; naturalization creates eligibility for the clearance
process, as it does for all citizens.

## 5. Competitive Analysis: Why the APV Wins

The global competition for high-net-worth immigrants is real and intensifying.
The UAE alone granted 35,000+ Golden Visas in 2023. Portugal's Golden Visa attracted
over €7B in investment before program modifications in 2023. Singapore's GIP is
oversubscribed and has tightened eligibility repeatedly.

**The APV's structural advantages over every competitor:**

**1. The dividend is unique.** No other Golden Visa program in the world offers an
ongoing financial return to the immigrant. Portugal gives residency; the APV gives
residency plus a growing annual dividend that compounds over decades. For a 45-year-old
investor who expects to live to 90, the present value of 27 years of growing dividends
($500-$2,000+/year in mature phase) can exceed the face value of the ASF contribution.

**2. The children's provision is unique.** No other program creates immediate, full
economic citizenship for American-born children of investors. It converts a temporary
immigration benefit into a multigenerational family integration.

**3. The U.S. economy is the largest.** The ASF dividend is a share in the world's
largest national economy. The fund's investment in U.S. equities, strategic sectors,
and commercial assets benefits from the scale and growth trajectory of the U.S. economy.
A Singaporean GIP investor's return is a share in a $500B economy; an APV investor's
long-run stake is a share in a $30T+ economy.

**4. Rule of law and property rights.** The U.S. has the world's strongest property
rights and rule of law infrastructure. A dividend owed by the U.S. government will
be paid; a benefit promised by a regime in political transition may not be.

## 6. Political Economy

### 6.1 Coalition Map

**Supportive:**
- ASF growth advocates: every APV contribution grows the fund that pays dividends to
  existing American citizens; the program is in every eligible American's financial interest
- Business community: Tier 2 APV brings skilled professionals in DED-priority sectors
  (semiconductors, AI, biotech); addresses the skilled worker shortage without H-1B caps
- Pro-immigration moderates: a merit-based, structured pathway with clear requirements,
  financial commitment, and integration incentives is the strongest available response
  to "immigration without commitment"
- Fiscal conservatives: the APV is self-financing (contributions fund the ASF) and
  generates positive fiscal externalities (high-income immigrants generate tax revenue,
  create businesses, employ Americans)
- National security hawks: the APV is more secure than EB-5 (stricter financial
  verification, CFIUS-adjacent review, restricted country enhanced screening)

**Opposed or skeptical:**
- Restrictionists: any immigration expansion is opposed; counter: this is the most
  rigorous and merit-based immigration pathway available; it is not amnesty or chain migration
- Labor protectionists (Tier 2): concern that Tier 2 APV displaces U.S. workers in
  high-skill sectors; counter: DED-priority sectors have documented shortages, not surpluses
- EB-5 industry: existing regional center operators lose business as APV replaces the
  functional demand for investor visas; this is the correct outcome given EB-5's fraud history

### 6.2 The Anti-Plutocracy Framing

The most important political challenge is the perception that the APV is a "citizenship
for sale" program that privileges the wealthy. The counter-framing:

**It is not citizenship for sale.** Citizenship requires naturalization, which requires
5 years of residency, a civics test, English proficiency, and a demonstrated commitment
to American values. The APV is a residency pathway, not a citizenship shortcut. The
investment accelerates the residency clock; it does not bypass the naturalization process.

**Every American benefits.** APV contributions grow the ASF. A larger ASF means a larger
dividend for every eligible American. The wealthy immigrant's contribution is literally
paid out to existing American citizens as a growing annual dividend. This is not
wealth concentration — it is wealth redistribution from incoming capital to existing
citizen-owners.

**Tier 2 is not wealth-based.** A semiconductor engineer earning $600,000/year in Taiwan
qualifies for Tier 2 APV on income and critical skill, not wealth. The program rewards
contribution to the American economy, not merely the possession of assets.

## 7. Integration with Existing Immigration Framework

### Relationship to EB-5

The APV is not a replacement for EB-5 — it is a competing pathway that addresses EB-5's
failures. Both programs can coexist. EB-5 retains its job-creation requirement and
regional center structure for investors who prefer that model. The APV offers a simpler,
faster, and more financially rational alternative.

**Expected outcome:** Most high-quality investor immigrants will prefer APV over EB-5
due to faster processing, simpler structure, and the ASF dividend incentive. EB-5
regional centers that survive will serve investors for whom the job-creation requirement
is a genuine goal rather than a visa mechanism.

### Relationship to H-1B

The APV-2 (human capital tier) addresses the same high-skill immigration demand as H-1B
but without the lottery system, employer sponsorship dependency, or numerical caps that
make H-1B an unreliable pathway. An AI researcher who cannot secure an H-1B lottery
slot can qualify for APV-2 on income and critical skill — and arrives with permanent
residency rather than a temporary work authorization.

**This is intentional.** H-1B's dependency structure (immigrant depends on employer
sponsorship, creating leverage for wage suppression and difficult work conditions) is
a labor market distortion. The APV-2's investor structure creates independence:
the immigrant's status is tied to their investment and skill, not to a specific employer.

## 8. Implementation

### Enabling Legislation

The APV requires amendments to the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) to create
the new visa category, and a separate provision in the American Sovereign Fund Act
(or enabling legislation) to create the ASF contribution mechanism and dividend accrual
clock for APV holders.

**Key INA provisions needed:**
- New INA §204(a)(1)(H): APV-1 classification and requirements
- New INA §204(a)(1)(I): APV-2 classification and requirements
- Amendment to INA §316 (naturalization requirements): APV years count toward residency
  requirement at defined rate
- Amendment to INA §320 (children's citizenship): clarification that APV parents' status
  does not affect children's birthright citizenship (already guaranteed by 14th Amendment
  but explicit statutory clarity reduces litigation)

**ASF Act provision:**
- New Section 4.5: APV holder dividend eligibility accrual at 1:1 (Tier 1) and 0.5:1
  (Tier 2) rates; accrual converts to full citizenship dividend upon naturalization
- New Section 4.6: American-born children of APV holders receive full 18-year dividend
  clock from date of birth (consistent with all natural-born citizens)

### Processing Infrastructure

**Administering agency:** USCIS (primary) + State Department (overseas applications) +
DHS national security screening + Treasury/FinCEN (source-of-funds verification)

**Processing target:** 90 days for complete, clean applications. Requires:
- Dedicated APV processing unit at USCIS (500-700 additional staff)
- Digital application portal with integrated financial verification
- CFIUS-adjacent review integration for restricted country nationals
- ASF contribution escrow mechanism (funds held in escrow during processing; released
  to ASF on approval; returned on denial)

**Annual cost of administration:** $150-250M (processing costs offset by $500-2,500 application fees)
