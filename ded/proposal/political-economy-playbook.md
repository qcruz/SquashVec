## Overview

The DED's proposals are technically sound and economically justified. That is necessary
but not sufficient for enactment. Every proposal creates winners and losers. Every proposal
will be opposed by organized interests that benefit from the status quo. Every proposal must
navigate a legislative process designed to give organized minorities veto power over majority-
benefit programs.

This document maps the political economy of each major DED proposal: who benefits, who is
threatened, what the organizing opposition looks like, and what coalitions can move the proposal
through Congress. It is the legislative strategy layer that translates technical policy design
into enacted law.
