# DED Red Team Scenario: Mississippi River System Extended Closure

**Scenario ID:** RT-005
**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Primary DED Scenarios Activated:** Drought, Shipping Blockade, Oil Restriction, Financial Fragmentation
**Estimated Economic Impact:** $80–200B over 12–18 months
**DED Readiness Assessment:** Good — multiple existing programs directly applicable; this is the
  scenario the DED is arguably most well-prepared for given existing drought and shipping instruments

---

## Overview

A combination of record low water levels (drought-driven) and a catastrophic lock failure at
the Mel Price Locks and Dam (Mississippi River, Alton IL — the critical chokepoint above St. Louis)
closes the upper Mississippi to commercial barge traffic for 8–14 months. The Mel Price locks
are the only navigation structure separating the upper and lower river systems; their failure
halts all grain movement from the Corn Belt to Gulf export terminals.

This scenario tests whether the DED's drought and shipping blockade instruments are actually
sufficient when they activate simultaneously, or whether their interaction creates gaps neither
scenario individually identified.
