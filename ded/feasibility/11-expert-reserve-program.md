## Feasibility Ratings

| Dimension | Rating | Summary |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Legal / Constitutional | **Strong** | DARPA Other Transaction Authority (OTA) is the direct legal model; national laboratory cooperation authority exists under DOE; expert reserve call-up provisions require clear voluntary-only framework |
| Administrative | **Moderate** | DARPA's program manager model is the administrative design; small team with high autonomy; recruiting competing-company experts requires confidentiality and IP firewalls |
| Economic | **Strong** | $84B investment → $31-99B/year in projected IP/licensing revenue (from value-discovery.md); DARPA's historical return is the benchmark; Accelerator programs generate IP retained by government |
| Political | **Moderate** | "Classified research with competing private sector experts" raises IP and competition concerns; DARPA model is the credibility anchor; Los Alamos model provides the historical precedent |
| Implementation | **Moderate** | Recruiting program managers with DARPA-equivalent authority and judgment is the critical bottleneck; Heilmeier Catechism is the evaluation framework |
| International Precedent | **Strong** | DARPA (U.S.), ARPA-E, ARIA (UK, 2023), BARDA, IARPA — the ARPA model has been replicated internationally; DED Accelerators are explicitly modeled on DARPA |

**Overall Implementation Readiness: Conditional** (requires DED and national lab cooperation agreements)
