## Feasibility Ratings

| Dimension | Rating | Summary |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Legal / Constitutional | **Strong** | The CRS framework is an analytical tool, not a regulatory instrument; it generates recommendations, not mandates; no novel legal authority required |
| Administrative | **Moderate** | Requires NEID data infrastructure and a team of supply chain analysts; existing data sources (BLS, Census, USITC, EIA) provide most inputs; HHI calculation is automated once data pipeline exists |
| Economic | **Strong** | The EAL framework produces CBO-scorable actuarial estimates; $56-190B/year in uninsured supply chain risk; the framework is the analytical foundation for all DED intervention prioritization |
| Political | **Strong** | A transparent, formula-driven risk assessment framework is difficult to politicize; the CRS scores are derived from documented data, not political designation |
| Implementation | **Moderate** | Data pipeline construction is the critical path; 18-24 months to operational NEID; initial CRS scores can be manually calculated before full automation |
| International Precedent | **Strong** | EU Critical Raw Materials Act (2023) uses similar concentration risk analysis; NATO supply chain resilience assessments; DHS CISA Critical Infrastructure Risk methodology |

**Overall Implementation Readiness: Conditional** (requires NEID data infrastructure)
