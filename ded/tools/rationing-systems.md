# DED Scope Note: Rationing Systems

**Category:** Out of scope — DED does not implement rationing
**Last Updated:** 2026-05

---

## Why This Is Not a DED Tool

Rationing — the allocation of scarce goods by authority rather than by price —
is implemented by FEMA, USDA, state emergency management agencies, and the
Office of the President under the Defense Production Act. It is an operational
crisis management instrument.

The DED's mandate is economic and fiscal intervention, not operational crisis
management. By the time a commodity shortage is severe enough to require rationing,
the window for DED's primary instruments — economic incentives, strategic contracts,
pre-positioned capacity — has already passed.

**DED's approach to shortage prevention:**
Rather than planning for rationing, the DED pre-positions the economic conditions
that make rationing unnecessary:

| Rationing Scenario | DED Instrument That Prevents It |
|--------------------|--------------------------------|
| Fuel shortage | Type A readiness contracts with refiners; SPR-adjacent reserve contracts; demand reduction via price incentives |
| Food shortage | Type C grain reserve contracts; OPP-01 crop genetics development; crop transition payments |
| Water shortage | Drip irrigation conversion incentives; desalination Type B contracts; CRHP infrastructure deployment |
| Medical supply shortage | Type A domestic API manufacturing readiness; SNS stockpile reserve contracts |
| Semiconductor shortage | Type A domestic fab readiness contracts; CHIPS Act follow-on Type B contracts |

In each case, the DED instrument addresses the supply gap at the economic level —
maintaining or expanding production capacity — so that the commodity is available
at manageable cost rather than unavailable at any price.

---

## The Correct DED Tool: Price Stabilization

Where commodity prices spike — which is the economic signal of shortage before
physical unavailability — the DED's relevant instruments are:

- **Type C strategic reserve contracts:** First-call rights on privately held
  inventory at pre-agreed pricing; releases into the market at the trigger price
  to dampen the spike without permanently distorting market incentives
- **Emergency tax credits / consumption rebates:** Targeted transfers to
  low-income households to maintain purchasing power when commodity prices rise,
  rather than suppressing the price signal entirely
- **Demand-side financial incentives:** Tax credits and financing programs that
  make demand reduction (efficiency, substitution) economically attractive before
  the price spike becomes a crisis

See `ded/tools/strategic-contracts.md` for the Type C reserve contract structure.
See `ded/tools/stimulus-checks.md` for the household income transfer instruments.
