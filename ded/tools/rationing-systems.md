**DED Tool Rating:** Tier A — Essential during acute shortage; no alternative allocation mechanism

---

## Overview

Rationing is the allocation of a scarce good by authority rather than by price. When
a critical commodity is severely constrained — fuel, water, food staples, medical supplies —
price-based allocation concentrates access among the wealthy and leaves essential functions
underfunded. A hospital cannot outbid a speculator for diesel in a crisis. A low-income
family cannot outbid a hoarder for canned goods.

The DED maintains pre-built rationing frameworks for every Tier 1 scenario commodity.
Rationing is a last-resort tool — it requires administrative capacity, enforcement, and
public compliance that are difficult to sustain. But when the alternative is uncontrolled
shortage with unequal access, rationing is the correct instrument.
