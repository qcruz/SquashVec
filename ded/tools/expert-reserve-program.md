# DED Tool Analysis: Expert Reserve Program (ERP)

**Category:** Labor Mobilization / Strategic Contracts (Type D)
**Last Updated:** 2026-05
**Relevant Scenarios:** All scenarios — cross-cutting human capital lever
**Related Tools:** [strategic-contracts.md](strategic-contracts.md) | [stimulus-checks.md](stimulus-checks.md)
**DED Tool Rating:** Tier S — Foundational; no equivalent exists in current federal architecture

---

## 1. Mechanism

The Expert Reserve Program recruits high-value civilian experts — across all fields and
all levels, from working engineers to C-suite executives — into a civilian-military hybrid
reserve status. In exchange for a salary supplement and associated benefits, members accept
periodic drilling obligations, emergency mobilization eligibility, and a primary loyalty
obligation to national security that supersedes their corporate employment during active service.

The program does three things simultaneously:

1. **Maintains a reserve of irreplaceable expertise** available for national emergency
   deployment without waiting for ad hoc recruitment under crisis conditions

2. **Builds a cross-industry, cross-institutional knowledge network** whose members
   have trained together, know each other's capabilities, and can form effective
   working groups quickly — before the emergency that requires them

3. **Creates the human infrastructure for concentrated national R&D programs** modeled
   on the Manhattan Project: isolated, resourced, cross-disciplinary teams working on
   problems of national importance, operating under the legal and operational cover of
   legitimate military training exercises

The program is not a draft. Enrollment is voluntary. The salary supplement is the
incentive; the reserve obligation is the exchange. Members retain their civilian careers
entirely outside of drilling and mobilization periods.

## 2. The Los Alamos Model

The Manhattan Project remains the most successful concentrated R&D program in history.
In 36 months, a group of recruited scientists — many working at universities, private
labs, and in some cases for competing institutions — produced a technological achievement
that no single institution, working independently, could have reached.

The conditions that made it work:
- **Concentration:** the participants were physically co-located, removing the friction
  of distributed collaboration
- **Resourcing:** they had whatever they needed; no budget fights, no procurement delays
- **Classification:** secrecy removed competitive pressure; participants could share
  everything with each other without fear of IP loss to a rival employer
- **Mission clarity:** everyone knew exactly what they were working toward
- **Cross-disciplinary composition:** physicists, engineers, chemists, mathematicians,
  and logisticians all in the same facility, solving each other's problems in real time
- **Legitimacy:** the work was structured as national military service, which resolved
  the corporate loyalty tension entirely

The DED replicates this model as a standing program, not a one-time emergency mobilization.
**DED Accelerator Programs** are intensive, time-bounded, classified collaboration events
that are simultaneously genuine military training exercises and genuine R&D programs.
The training is real. The national security output is real. Neither is cover for the other —
they are the same activity conducted at the same time.

## 3. Enrollment Structure

### Eligibility Criteria

ERP is not open to the general public. It targets individuals whose knowledge,
experience, or position makes them uniquely valuable to a DED scenario response.

**Primary target categories:**

| Category | Why Targeted | Example Roles |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Advanced technology leadership | Carry institutional and technical knowledge that cannot be quickly replicated | Chief AI Officer, VP Engineering at semiconductor company, lead reactor designer |
| Critical infrastructure operators | Know the actual systems, not the theoretical ones | Grid control room supervisors, pipeline operations directors, port logistics chiefs |
| Life sciences and public health | Pandemic response requires experienced practitioners, not students of pandemics | Chief Medical Officers, senior virologists, vaccine manufacturing directors |
| Agricultural science and operations | Crop genetics, soil science, and farm operations expertise is deep and slow to develop | Seed company chief scientists, agricultural extension leads, precision ag developers |
| Financial system operators | The people who actually run clearing systems, risk desks, and commodity exchanges | CME operations directors, Fed trading desk supervisors, SWIFT network engineers |
| Cyber and infrastructure security | Defensive capability is personal — it lives in individual expertise, not in tools | CISA-cleared security researchers, grid SCADA specialists, critical infrastructure red teamers |

**Explicit design principle — competing companies:**
ERP deliberately recruits from competing institutions in the same sector. This serves
three purposes:

1. **Redundancy:** If one company fails, goes bankrupt, or is acquired by a foreign entity,
   the expertise is not lost from the reserve
2. **Diversity:** No single company's architecture, approach, or blind spot dominates
   the reserve's knowledge base
3. **Loyalty alignment:** Members recruited from competing companies share a primary
   loyalty — national security — that is explicitly above their corporate loyalty during
   service. This is constitutionally established: military service supersedes corporate
   employment obligations (USERRA). The ERP extends this principle to the DED reserve.

A team assembled from Google, Anthropic, Meta, and OpenAI AI researchers, serving together
in a DED Accelerator, has no company-protective incentive to withhold knowledge from
each other. Classification removes the IP concern. The mission is shared.

### Compensation Structure

**Annual Salary Supplement:**
ERP members receive a federal salary supplement paid regardless of whether they are
called up in a given year. This is the retainer — the price of maintaining their
availability and primary reserve loyalty.

| Member Tier | Criteria | Annual Supplement |
|-------------|----------|------------------|
| Tier 1 — Specialist | Deep technical expertise in a scenario-critical domain; individual contributor | $15,000–$40,000/year |
| Tier 2 — Senior Expert | Director-level or equivalent; leads teams; institutional knowledge holder | $40,000–$80,000/year |
| Tier 3 — Principal | C-suite, named technical lead, or acknowledged field authority | $80,000–$150,000/year |

These figures are calibrated to be meaningful but not dominant relative to senior civilian
salaries. A Chief AI Officer earning $800,000/year receives a $100,000 supplement — a 12.5%
increase that is both financially meaningful and symbolically significant as recognition
of national service status.

**Training Day Compensation:**
Same as CRHP: active-duty equivalent pay rate for each training day served (~$500–$1,200/day
at E-7 to O-4 equivalent rates for ERP member ranks).

**Additional Benefits:**
- TRICARE healthcare coverage (same as CRHP; see overview.md)
- Security clearance at appropriate level (TS/SCI where required by assignment)
- Educational benefit: up to $25,000/year tuition for degree or professional development
  programs relevant to the member's ERP track
- Retention credit: members who complete 10 consecutive years of ERP service receive a
  federal pension credit equivalent to 5 years of active military service

---

### Obligations

**Annual Training:**
- 10 days of DED training annually (standard year; no activation)
- At least one DED Accelerator Program participation every 3 years
- Quarterly readiness certification: confirm availability, update contact and skills records,
  review any scenario-relevant classified briefings

**Emergency Mobilization:**
- Available for up to 120 days/year of active DED service in a declared emergency
- 14-day advance notice standard; 72-hour notice in acute emergency
- Civilian employment protected under USERRA extension applicable to DED reserve
- Employer notification: ERP members inform their employer of reserve status at enrollment;
  employer cannot terminate, demote, or disadvantage member for reserve obligations

**Loyalty Provision:**
- During active DED service, the member's primary obligation is to national security
- Members may not share classified work product with their civilian employer
- Members may not use DED-developed work product for competitive commercial advantage
- Violation is treated as a federal security breach, not a civilian employment matter

## 4. DED Accelerator Programs

### Concept

A DED Accelerator is an intensive, residential, classified collaboration event bringing
together 20–200 ERP members from across sectors and institutions. Duration: 2 weeks to
6 months depending on the program. Structure: part military training exercise, part
concentrated R&D sprint, unified by a single national security objective.

The dual nature is not a deception — it is the design. The training exercise component
is genuine: participants practice emergency coordination, decision-making under pressure,
cross-organizational communication, and scenario response. The R&D component is also
genuine: the same participants, working together under classification, make real progress
on problems of national importance that no single institution could solve alone.

The classification framework is what makes this possible. Under security classification:
- Participants can share proprietary knowledge without fear of IP loss to a competitor
- Work product belongs to the government (as with any classified work), eliminating
  competitive advantage concerns
- Participants are legally protected from demands by their employers to disclose what
  they worked on
- The government retains and can deploy the resulting research

### Program Examples

---

#### ACCELERATOR ALPHA — AI Alignment and National Security

**Objective:** Advance the state of AI safety and alignment research, with specific focus
on scenarios where AI systems are critical to national infrastructure or defense — and
where misalignment would constitute a national security threat.

**Participants:** 60–80 ERP members drawn from:
- AI research leads at OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI (competing companies;
  together they cover the full state of the art)
- DoD AI researchers (DARPA, NSA, JAIC)
- Academic AI safety researchers (MIT, Stanford, CMU, UC Berkeley)
- Cybersecurity researchers (NSA, CISA, PNNL)
- Policy and legal experts (for governance framework development)

**Training exercise component:** AI-enabled infrastructure attack scenario; participants
must diagnose, contain, and recover from a simulated AI system failure in critical
infrastructure. Exercise develops cross-organizational emergency response protocols.

**R&D component:** Under classification, participants work on:
- Shared safety evaluation frameworks that no single lab currently has incentive to
  develop (because developing them exposes proprietary model weaknesses)
- AI behavior in adversarial environments relevant to national security
- National AI incident response playbooks
- Interpretability tools for assessing AI systems integrated into critical infrastructure

**Output:** Classified technical report; national AI security standards; potentially,
a set of safety tools that are subsequently open-sourced or licensed to allied nations
under DED value-discovery framework (OPP structure).

**Duration:** 90 days, residential, at a designated national laboratory facility.

---

#### ACCELERATOR BRAVO — Nuclear Fusion Engineering Sprint

**Objective:** Accelerate the engineering challenges blocking commercial nuclear fusion
deployment. Fusion has achieved scientific ignition (LLNL NIF, 2022); the remaining
barriers are engineering, materials, and manufacturing — not physics.

**Participants:** 40–60 ERP members drawn from:
- Commonwealth Fusion, TAE Technologies, Helion Energy, NIF (competing fusion companies)
- ORNL, Argonne, INL (national lab materials and nuclear engineering expertise)
- Specialty materials manufacturers (high-temperature superconductors, plasma-facing materials)
- Grid integration engineers (how does a fusion plant connect to the grid?)
- Naval nuclear reactor engineers (decades of relevant small-reactor engineering experience)

**Training exercise component:** Nuclear facility emergency response exercise; simulated
reactor incident requiring cross-organizational technical coordination. Genuine training
for real emergency response.

**R&D component:** Concentrated work on shared engineering bottlenecks:
- High-temperature superconducting magnet manufacturing at commercial scale
- Plasma-facing component materials under sustained fusion conditions
- Tritium breeding and handling systems
- Compact heat exchanger design for fusion thermal output

**Why this works under classification:** Each private fusion company is racing the others;
none will share engineering solutions openly. Under classification, the competitive barrier
is removed. The government retains the output; companies receive the benefit of accelerated
shared engineering knowledge. The national interest (commercial fusion as fast as possible)
aligns everyone.

**Duration:** 60 days, at ORNL or INL fusion research facility.

---

#### ACCELERATOR CHARLIE — Agricultural Genetic Engineering

**Objective:** Develop the advanced crop strain IP that forms the basis of DED value
discovery opportunity OPP-01 — drought-tolerant, nitrogen-efficient staple crop varieties.

**Participants:** 30–50 ERP members drawn from:
- Seed company scientists: Bayer (formerly Monsanto), Corteva, Syngenta (competing companies;
  collectively hold the world's deepest private plant genetics expertise)
- USDA Agricultural Research Service
- Land-grant university plant geneticists (Iowa State, Purdue, UC Davis)
- Precision fermentation and synthetic biology researchers
- International agricultural scientists from allied nations (under security agreements)

**Training exercise component:** Food security crisis simulation; participants manage a
scenario in which a major crop failure requires rapid substitution, stockpile management,
and alternative production coordination.

**R&D component:**
- Development of next-generation drought-tolerant corn and wheat germplasm
- Nitrogen-use-efficiency trait development and validation
- Disease-resistance stacking for climate-shifted pathogen environments
- CRISPR-based editing protocols that can be applied to existing elite varieties

**IP structure:** All work product is jointly owned by the U.S. government and participating
institutions under pre-agreed terms. Government retains the licensing rights described
in OPP-01. Participating companies receive a perpetual royalty-free domestic license for
commercial application — providing them direct commercial value as an enrollment incentive.

**Duration:** 90 days; can run as recurring annual programs as the breeding program matures.

---

#### ACCELERATOR DELTA — Grid Defense and Infrastructure Security

**Objective:** Develop the next generation of grid cybersecurity protocols and hardened
control systems that are immediately deployable to U.S. utilities and allied grid operators.

**Participants:** 50–70 ERP members drawn from:
- Grid operators: PJM, MISO, CAISO, SPP, ERCOT (the major ISOs/RTOs)
- Utility companies: Dominion, Duke, Con Ed, Xcel, AEP (competing private operators)
- PNNL, Sandia, INL (national lab grid security leads)
- ICS/SCADA security researchers
- DoD Cyber Command, NSA, CISA

**Training exercise component:** Full grid attack simulation — participants manage a
coordinated physical + cyber attack on multiple grid segments simultaneously. This is
the most realistic grid attack exercise ever conducted because the participants are the
actual people who operate the real systems.

**R&D component:**
- Development of anomaly detection protocols that work across heterogeneous grid control
  systems (the real grid uses 40+ different SCADA systems; interoperability is the gap)
- Hardened control system designs for EHV transformer replacement
- Incident response playbooks that work across ISO/utility boundaries
- Red team testing of existing grid control architectures with findings classified
  (so vulnerabilities are not publicly disclosed before they are patched)

**Duration:** 45 days; exercises repeated annually with new participants from the ERP roster.

### Program Administration

**Selection for Accelerators:**
DED Research Coordination Office maintains a skills registry of all ERP members. When
an Accelerator is planned, a selection board (chaired by relevant national laboratory
director + DED Research Deputy Secretary) identifies and invites participants based on:
- Skills match to the program objectives
- Prior Accelerator participation (ensure rotation; no permanent cohort)
- Security clearance level
- Availability within the drilling obligation calendar

**Facility:**
Accelerators are hosted at national laboratory facilities or designated secure DoD
installations. Participants live on-site for the duration (hotel equivalent housing
provided). Family visitation policy exists for programs longer than 30 days.

**Classification:**
- Program existence: Unclassified (training exercise programs are public)
- Participant list: Classified (to protect from foreign targeting)
- Work product: Classified at appropriate level; declassified on rolling basis as
  vulnerabilities are patched and technologies are deployed

**Employer Relations:**
The ERP employer notification system informs companies that their employee has been called
to a training exercise. Companies are not informed of the content. Companies cannot
require disclosure under USERRA protections. Companies are, in practice, informed that
their best people are working on national security — which most large companies find
reputationally valuable rather than threatening.

## 5. Interaction with Other DED Tools

### ERP + Strategic Contracts
Accelerator output often generates the IP and technical specifications that justify
subsequent Type B developmental contracts. The Accelerator produces the proof-of-concept;
the strategic contract funds the commercial-scale development.

### ERP + CRHP
ERP members are automatically enrolled in CRHP at full coverage (no fee; coverage is part
of the ERP benefit package). ERP members with medical backgrounds serve as Medical Reserve
track leads within CRHP — the elite knowledge tier within the broader reserve.

### ERP + National Laboratory Network
National lab staff who meet ERP eligibility criteria are natural candidates. Labs benefit
from the cross-industry exposure; industry benefits from the lab research access.
Accelerator programs blur the boundary between government and private research in
exactly the way that produces breakthrough outcomes.

## 6. Historical Precedents

| Program | Relevance |
|---------|-----------|
| **Manhattan Project (1942–1945)** | Direct model: recruited competing academics and scientists; classified collaboration; produced irreplaceable national security output in compressed timeframe |
| **JASON Defense Advisory Group (1960–present)** | Academic scientists under DoD contract, providing classified technical analysis; ERP is this model scaled to operational practitioners |
| **Interagency Personnel Act (1970)** | Allows federal agencies to bring in private sector experts for up to 2 years; ERP is a permanent version with stronger incentive structure |
| **DARPA Program Manager model** | DARPA recruits world-class experts for 4-year terms; they direct research programs then return to private sector; ERP is a lighter version with longer retention |
| **Selective Service System** | Administrative infrastructure for mobilizing civilian population; ERP uses analogous legal framework but for voluntary specialized reserve |
| **Naval Reserve Merchant Marine** | WWII program converting civilian merchant sailors to naval reserve status; service obligation in exchange for military benefits and protections |

## 7. Risks and Failure Modes

### Corporate Resistance
**Risk:** Employers refuse to accommodate reserve obligations; use informal means to
disadvantage ERP members (passed over for promotion, excluded from key projects).

**Mitigation:** USERRA enforcement is federal; penalties for retaliation are substantial.
ERP employer certification: companies with >1,000 employees must certify USERRA compliance
and ERP accommodation as a condition of receiving any DED strategic contract. Alignment
of interests: companies whose employees are in ERP gain preferred status in Type D
contract solicitations — making ERP enrollment a competitive advantage for employers.

### Foreign Intelligence Targeting
**Risk:** The ERP member roster, if identified by adversaries, becomes a targeting list
for foreign intelligence recruitment, corporate espionage, or physical targeting.

**Mitigation:** Member roster is classified. Security training for all members; counterintelligence
briefings at enrollment and annually. Members flagged for unusual contact with foreign
nationals; enhanced monitoring during sensitive assignments.

### Accelerator Output Leakage
**Risk:** Participants return to their employers with classified knowledge and, intentionally
or inadvertently, allow it to influence competitive commercial decisions.

**Mitigation:** Classification training; polygraph option for highest-sensitivity programs;
firewall protocols (participants cannot work on projects directly related to classified
content for 12 months post-Accelerator). Violation is a federal security offense with
career-ending consequences — a credible deterrent.

### Elite Capture
**Risk:** The ERP becomes dominated by established incumbent companies and institutions,
excluding emerging competitors and independent researchers whose knowledge may be more
current or more innovative.

**Mitigation:** ERP enrollment quota by institution: no single company may have more than
5% of any Accelerator cohort. Independent researchers and academics are actively recruited.
Small company and startup track: companies with <200 employees have a reserved enrollment
pathway with lower drilling obligation and adjusted compensation to accommodate resource
constraints.
