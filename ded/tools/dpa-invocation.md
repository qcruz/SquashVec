## Overview

The Defense Production Act (DPA) of 1950 is the primary U.S. statute authorizing the executive branch
to direct private economic activity in the national interest. Originally designed for wartime
industrial mobilization, it has been invoked for pandemics (COVID-19), energy crises, and supply
chain disruptions. The DED's strategic contract framework (Types A/B/C/D) operates within and
alongside DPA authority — DPA provides the mandate; DED contracts provide the economic structure
that makes compliance voluntary and efficient rather than coercive and litigated.

This document analyzes DPA invocation as a DED policy tool: when to use it, how it interacts
with voluntary DED contract mechanisms, and how to design DPA orders that achieve economic
outcomes without triggering the legal and political resistance that poorly structured invocations
historically produce.
