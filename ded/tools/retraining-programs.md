## Overview

Retraining programs provide structured pathways for workers displaced from declining occupations
to acquire credentials and skills for growing occupations. The DED operates retraining programs
as the supply-side complement to income support instruments: stimulus and TRAL provide the income
floor that makes retraining economically feasible; retraining programs provide the credential
pathway that makes income support a bridge rather than a permanent subsidy.

DED retraining is distinct from traditional workforce development in two ways:
1. It is tied to NEID real-time occupation demand data — training slots are funded for occupations
   where demand is growing, not based on historical program inertia
2. It is the delivery mechanism for the ATC-funded benefit pipeline; retraining enrollment
   triggers the income support package (conditional displacement transfer from ATC revenue)
