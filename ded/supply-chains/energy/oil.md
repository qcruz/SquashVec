# Supply Chain Base Map: Oil and Liquid Fuels

> This is a scenario-agnostic dependency map. For scenario-specific analysis,
> see the relevant `scenarios/<name>/supply-chains/` files.

## What Oil Produces (Downstream Dependencies)

Oil is a **platform input** — it does not have one use, it has hundreds.
Disrupting oil disrupts all of these simultaneously.

| Output | Key Dependent Sectors |
|---|---|
| Gasoline | Personal vehicles, light trucks |
| Diesel | Freight trucking, rail, agriculture, construction, military |
| Jet fuel | Aviation (commercial and military) |
| Bunker fuel | Marine shipping |
| Heating oil | Residential/commercial heating (Northeast U.S.) |
| Naphtha | Petrochemical feedstock → plastics, fertilizer, pharmaceuticals |
| Propane / LPG | Crop drying, heating, industrial |
| Lubricants | All mechanical equipment |
| Asphalt | Road construction and maintenance |

## What Oil Requires (Upstream Dependencies)

| Input | Source Concentration | Substitution |
|---|---|---|
| Crude oil | Global market, OPEC+ concentrated | Strategic reserve, demand reduction |
| Refining capacity | U.S. refinery network (concentrated, aging) | Limited short-term |
| Pipeline network | Fixed infrastructure | Difficult to reroute |
| Shipping (tankers) | Global fleet | Some flexibility |
| Skilled refinery workforce | Specialized, aging workforce | Difficult short-term |

## Single Points of Failure

- **Gulf Coast refinery concentration:** ~50% of U.S. refining capacity is in hurricane-vulnerable Texas/Louisiana corridor
- **Strait of Hormuz:** ~20% of global oil supply passes through this single chokepoint
- **Colonial Pipeline:** Supplies ~45% of East Coast refined fuel — single corridor

## U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR)

- **Capacity:** ~714 million barrels (as of recent years, current fill TBD)
- **Daily U.S. consumption:** ~20 million barrels/day
- **Drawdown rate possible:** ~4 million barrels/day maximum
- **Duration at max drawdown:** ~180 days before exhaustion
- **Purpose:** Bridge time, not solve disruption

## Substitution Timeline

| Substitute | Applicability | Lead Time |
|---|---|---|
| Demand reduction (behavior) | All uses | Immediate |
| SPR release | All refined products | Days |
| Domestic production increase | Crude supply | 3–6 months |
| Coal-to-liquid (CTL) | Diesel, aviation fuel | 3–5 years to scale |
| EV transition | Personal vehicles | 5–15 years at scale |
| Biofuels | Limited volume | 1–3 years to scale |
| Synthetic aviation fuel | Aviation | 5–10 years to scale |
