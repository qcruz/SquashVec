# Supply Chain Base Map: Fertilizer

> Scenario-agnostic base map. See scenario files for disruption-specific analysis.

## The Core Dependency

Modern agriculture produces approximately 2x more calories per acre than
pre-synthetic-fertilizer agriculture. Removing synthetic fertilizer does not
return us to 1900 — it causes famine at current population levels.

## Nitrogen (Most Critical)

**Production process:** Haber-Bosch synthesis
```
Natural gas (H₂ source) + Atmospheric nitrogen (N₂) → Ammonia (NH₃)
Ammonia → Urea, ammonium nitrate, ammonium sulfate (crop-applicable forms)
```

**Energy intensity:** ~33 MMBtu per ton of ammonia — extremely energy-intensive.

**U.S. supply situation:**
- ~40% of nitrogen fertilizer is imported
- Major import sources: Canada, Russia, Trinidad and Tobago
- Domestic production: ~20 major facilities, concentrated in Gulf Coast and Midwest

## Phosphorus and Potassium

- **Phosphorus (P):** U.S. has significant domestic reserves (Florida, Idaho)
  but processing is concentrated. Global supply dominated by Morocco.
- **Potassium (K):** U.S. has minimal domestic production. ~90% imported,
  primarily from Canada (world's largest producer).

**Potassium is the most import-dependent of the three major nutrients.**

## Substitution Analysis

| Substitute | Viable Scale | Transition Time | Yield Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic reserve (6–18 mo) | High | Pre-built only | None |
| Domestic production expansion | Moderate | 2–4 years | None |
| Precision application (reduce waste) | High | Immediate | 10–15% efficiency gain |
| Organic nitrogen (manure, compost) | Low at national scale | 1–2 seasons | 20–40% yield reduction |
| Crop rotation (legumes fix N) | Moderate | 1 season | 15–25% yield reduction |
