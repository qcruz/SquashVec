# Supply Chain Base Map: Grain

> Scenario-agnostic base map. See scenario files for disruption-specific analysis.

## What Grain Produces

| Output | Key Uses |
|---|---|
| Wheat flour | Bread, pasta, food staples |
| Corn | Animal feed (~40%), ethanol (~40%), human food (~15%) |
| Soybeans | Animal feed, cooking oil, industrial uses |
| Rice | Staple food, concentrated in specific regions |
| Barley/oats | Animal feed, brewing, human food |

## Critical Dependencies

| Input | Concentration Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nitrogen fertilizer | High | Natural gas feedstock, partially imported |
| Diesel (farm equipment) | High | No near-term substitute |
| Freshwater (irrigation) | High | Aquifer depletion is long-term risk |
| Seed supply | Moderate | Consolidated to few suppliers |
| Crop protection chemicals | Moderate | Petroleum-derived |
| Rail/truck for distribution | High | Long distances from production to consumption |

## Geographic Concentration

U.S. grain production is highly concentrated in the Midwest corn belt.
A regional disruption (drought, flooding, infrastructure failure) affects
national supply significantly.

**Key vulnerability:** Most major population centers are 1,000–2,000 miles
from grain production. Distribution depends entirely on functioning freight
logistics.

## Substitution Options

| Scenario | Substitute | Lag |
|---|---|---|
| Corn shortage | Wheat, potatoes, other starches | 1 season |
| Wheat shortage | Corn, rice, other grains | 1 season |
| Distribution failure | Regional production increase | 2–3 seasons |
| Fertilizer shortage | Organic inputs, crop rotation | 1–3 seasons, yield loss |
